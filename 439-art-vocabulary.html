Home439

This is the deep vocabulary lesson for navigating Israeli art professionally — gallery wall texts, auction catalogues, museum tours, art-school crit sessions, Haaretz arts pages. We cover the technical vocabulary (medium, technique, support), institutional vocabulary (gallery, auction, biennial), critical vocabulary (figurative vs abstract, conceptual, installation), and price-and-buying vocabulary you need at Sotheby\'s Tel Aviv or Tiroche Auction House.

Cultural note — Israeli art collectors often cluster in two groups: tech-money in Tel Aviv (collecting contemporary, internationally-shown artists) and traditional families with multi-generational links to Bezalel-style work. Hebrew vocabulary covers both registers — knowing them lets you read a Tiroche sale catalogue and a Helena Rubinstein Pavilion wall text in the same afternoon.
