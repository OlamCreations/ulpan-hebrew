Crops, Seasons, and the Hemp Frontier

Lesson 177 · גִּדּוּלִים וְעוֹנוֹת
Home177

Israeli agriculture covers everything from biblical-era olives and wheat to high-tech tomatoes and exotic mango. Each crop has its season, its growing region, its irrigation needs. This lesson covers the major crops and seasonal calendar — plus the regulatory landscape for hemp/cannabis (תֻּעֲשִׂיָתִי), which is your direct frontier with Hemp of Negev.

Cultural note — Israel\'s "Tu BiShvat" (the New Year of Trees, January-February) is the traditional planting season — KKL plants thousands of trees on this day. The harvest calendar is biblical-based: barley harvest at Passover (March-April), wheat at Shavuot (May-June), grapes at Sukkot (Sept-Oct), olives in late autumn. Knowing this calendar makes you fluent in the agricultural rhythms of the country.
