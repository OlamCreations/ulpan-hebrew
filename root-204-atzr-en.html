Root ע־צ־ר: stopping

Roots Atlas 204 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ע צ ר · the stopping root
Home ע צ ר Morphology
ע צ ר
' · tz · r
everything to do with halting, holding back, detaining
6 words · 1 root · 1 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: to hold back. The same verb stops a car, presses oil from an olive, and locks a suspect in a cell, because all three are forms of stopping a flow. From it Hebrew builds the police term for an arrest, the medical term for being blocked up, and the festival name Atzeret, the day you "hold back" and stay one more night. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַעֲצֹרla'atzor
simple active, the plain action
to stop, to halt; to arrest, to detain; to press oil

This root lives mainly in the simple pa'al, which already covers a wide spread of meanings from halting traffic to making an arrest. The passive participle עָצוּר and the agent-like עָצִיר below carry the "held back" sense without needing a separate binyan.

Words born from the root
