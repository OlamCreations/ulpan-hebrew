Block 45 Summary — Architecture

Lesson 450 · סִכּוּם הָאַרְכִיטֶקְטוּרָה

Block 45 traced the country\'s built environment through nine lessons: Bauhaus / White City UNESCO 2003, the Jerusalem-stone Mandate code from 1918, Israeli brutalism (Knesset, Negev Brigade Monument, Hecker\'s spiral), Moshe Safdie\'s Yad Vashem prism + international career, the universal typology of Israeli daily life (synagogue, sukkah, mamad, apartment), the Tel Aviv boulevard as urban form, the Yad Vashem complex, the religious architecture of four traditions, and the deep working vocabulary of drawings + materials + planning. This lesson consolidates the seven essential names + ten essential terms.

Cultural note — A literate Israeli can identify a Bauhaus building by its horizontal windows and pilotis, recognise Jerusalem stone by colour, name the Knesset architects, point at a sukkah\'s s\'chach and tell you it must let stars through, and locate a mamad door at a glance. This block is not for becoming an architect. It is for moving through Israel as a literate inhabitant of its cities and villages.
