US-Israel Political Relations & AIPAC

Lesson 394 · יַחֲסֵי אַרְצוֹת הַבְּרִית-יִשְׂרָאֵל וְאַיְפַּ"ק
Home394

The US-Israel relationship is the most consequential bilateral alliance shaping Israeli political life. AIPAC (founded 1953 by Si Kenen, ~100K members) operates as the most effective foreign-policy lobby on Capitol Hill. J Street (2007) emerged as the progressive alternative. The Conference of Presidents (1956) coordinates 50+ Jewish organizations. ADL (1913), AJC (1906), JFNA, and HIAS form the institutional Jewish-American architecture. $3.8B/year military aid (since 1951, formalized post-1973), 1995 Jerusalem Embassy Act, 2018 embassy move, Birthright (1999, ~750K participants), and the growing generational divide — older Jews ~70% Democrat loyal to Israel, younger Jewish progressives increasingly critical post-2014 — define the modern landscape.

Cultural note — When Israelis say "ha-lobby" (הַלּוֹבִּי) they mean AIPAC almost exclusively. "J Street" stays in English. The phrase "yachasei artzot ha-brit" (US-Israel relations) is shorthand for the entire diplomatic-military-economic-cultural package. Younger Israelis are increasingly aware that "American Jews" is no longer a monolith — the generational fracture is now a recurring topic on Channel 12 and in Haaretz. Speak about it with nuance: the relationship is solid at the state level, contested at the community level.
