~50% of Israeli Jews are Mizrahi/Sephardi descent. Their kitchens dominate everyday Israeli food — Moroccan Friday couscous, Iraqi sabich, Tunisian shakshuka, Persian gondi, Yemeni jachnun, Libyan mafrum. Each tradition has its own bread, its own Friday meal, its own spice mix. Lesson 18 (block 18) will cover Mizrahi music & culture more broadly; this lesson is food-only.

Cultural note — Mizrahi cuisines were marginalized in early Israel — the dominant Ashkenazi establishment considered them "primitive." That changed in the 1970s with the Black Panthers and continues today: chefs like Eyal Shani, Uri Buri, Erez Komarovsky, Ezra Kedem helped legitimize Mizrahi food in fine dining. The shift mirrors the broader Mizrahi cultural assertion since the 1990s.
