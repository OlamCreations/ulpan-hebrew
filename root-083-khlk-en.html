Root ח־ל־ק — dividing

Roots Atlas 83 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ח ל ק · the dividing root
Home ח ל ק Morphology
ח ל ק
ch · l · k
everything to do with splitting into parts
10 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

One idea runs under all these words: a whole cut into parts. Divide a cake and each person gets a chelek (part); divide a number and you do chiluk (division); divide opinions and you reach a machloket (disagreement). The same root even gives smooth (חָלָק): a surface with no parts sticking out, nothing to catch on. Splitting and smoothness are the two faces of the same cut. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַחֲלֹקlachalok
simple active — the plain action
to share, to divide; to disagree
Pi'el
לְחַלֵּקlechalek
intensive / specialized active
to share out, to distribute, to divide
Hif'il
לְהַחֲלִיקlehachalik
causative — make someone do it
to slip, to slide; to make smooth

Notice the split inside the verbs themselves: pa'al and pi'el both mean to divide (pi'el is the active, hands-on distributing), while hif'il jumps to the other sense of the root, smoothness, hence to slip or glide. The passive partners (nif'al, pu'al, huf'al) and the reflexive hitpa'el are not lexicalized here.
