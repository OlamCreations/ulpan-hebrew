Bialik — "El Ha-Tzipor" (To the Bird, excerpt)

Hayyim Nahman Bialik's first published poem (1891). Asks a returning bird about the Land of Israel. The opening of modern Hebrew poetry.

Amichai — "My Father" (אבי, excerpt)

Yehuda Amichai (1924-2000), the most translated modern Hebrew poet. Plain-spoken, intimate, secular-spiritual. This poem is taught in every Israeli high school.

Rachel — "Maybe" (אולי, excerpt)

Rachel Bluwstein (1890-1931), the poetess of Kinneret. Wrote in the lake landscape that became myth. Died young from tuberculosis. This is her most famous poem.

Lea Goldberg — "Pine" (אורן, excerpt)

Lea Goldberg (1911-1970). The pain of the immigrant — having two homelands and belonging to neither. Universally relatable for olim.

Naomi Shemer — "Yerushalayim Shel Zahav" (Jerusalem of Gold)

Written by Naomi Shemer in 1967, weeks before the Six-Day War. After the reunification of Jerusalem, became Israel's unofficial second anthem. Refrain below.
