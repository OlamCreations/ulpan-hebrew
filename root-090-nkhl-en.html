Root נ־ח־ל: inheritance

Roots Atlas 90 · שֹׁרֶשׁ נ ח ל · the inheriting root
Home נ ח ל Morphology
נ ח ל
n · ch · l
everything to do with inheriting and settling land
6 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root, two faces that turn out to be one. The biblical sense is to inherit, and the inherited thing is the נַחֲלָה, the family estate. But the same letters name the נַחַל, the streambed that carves the land you inherit, and the act of settling on it. Inheritance, land, and the water that shapes it all braid into one idea: the portion that becomes yours. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִנְחֹלlinchol
simple active, the plain action
to inherit, to gain
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְנַחֵלlehitnachel
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to settle, to inhabit (land)

Pa'al לִנְחֹל is the original "receive a portion as your own" (today also "to win, to suffer" a defeat or victory). Hitpa'el לְהִתְנַחֵל turns that into the act of physically settling the land, and feeds the loaded modern noun הִתְנַחֲלוּת. The passive and causative binyanim are not lexicalized here; the root works through pa'al, hitpa'el, and its rich set of nouns.
