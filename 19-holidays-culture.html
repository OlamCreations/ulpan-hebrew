Israeli life revolves around the Hebrew calendar. Knowing the holidays, their greetings, and symbols is essential to integrate. Includes Shabbat, holy sites, and everyday Jewish life vocabulary.

Calendar — Major Holidays

Yom Tov — "good day" = holiday. On Yom Tov no work is done (like Shabbat). These are: Rosh Hashana, Yom Kippur, Sukkot, Pessach, Shavuot.

Shabbat Vocabulary

Shabbat — From Friday sundown to Saturday sundown. Everything closes: public transit, shops, restaurants (except some in Tel Aviv). The country stops. Prepare everything before Friday 4 PM.

Pessach — Mah Nishtana (Four Questions)

Sung by the youngest child at the seder. Every Israeli kid learns this by age 5. The 4 questions about why this night is different.

Hanukkah — Maoz Tsur (excerpt)

Sung after lighting the candles. 13th century Ashkenazic hymn. First verse below.

Yom HaAtzmaut — Hatikva (National Anthem)

Israel's national anthem. Lyrics by Naftali Herz Imber (1878). Sung at every official ceremony.

Rosh Hashana — Apple in Honey Blessing

Said before dipping the apple in honey at the Rosh Hashana meal. Wishes for a sweet year.

Hanukkah — Sevivon Sov Sov Sov (children's song)

The dreidel song every Israeli child learns. Played while spinning the sevivon.
