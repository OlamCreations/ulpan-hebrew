Root ח־מ־מ — heat

Roots Atlas 47 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ח מ מ · the heat root
Home ח מ מ Morphology
ח מ מ
ch · m · m
everything to do with being warm
8 words · 1 root · 1 verb-voices

This is a geminate root: the second and third letters are the same מ, so it often looks like just two letters at work. One idea runs through all of it: heat. From it you get the bare adjective חַם (hot), the abstract noun חֹם (heat itself), the verb that makes things warm, the place where you keep things warm (a greenhouse), and even the sun as the hot one. Notice how the niqqud and the doubled מ shift the same three consonants from quality, to substance, to action, to place. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְחַמֵּםlechamem
intensive / specialized active
to heat, to warm up

In everyday Hebrew the verb of this root lives almost entirely in the active pi'el לְחַמֵּם. Its natural partners exist too: the passive pu'al (to be heated) and the reflexive hitpa'el לְהִתְחַמֵּם (to warm oneself up, to warm up before sport), whose action-noun הִתְחַמְּמוּת appears below. The plain pa'al is not lexicalized for this root.

Words born from the root
