The Reserves — Miluim

Lesson 156 · מִלּוּאִים
Home156

Miluim — the reserves — are the IDF\'s defining structural feature. After 32 months of mandatory service, Israeli men remain in reserves up to age 40+ (combat) or 55 (non-combat). Annual call-up: 2-6 weeks. After Oct 7, 2023: 360,000 reservists called up — largest mobilization since Yom Kippur. The reserves are also a defining strain on Israeli families and businesses.

Cultural note — "צַו 8" — Order 8 — is the emergency call-up. Most Israelis never hear it; on Oct 7, 2023, every reservist did. The reserves bond is intense — small unit groups stay together for decades. The phrase "מִלּוּאִים — תּוֹדָה" — "Miluim — thanks" — is what you say to a reservist back from duty. Some people get reserve service as a badge; some as a burden. Both are true.
