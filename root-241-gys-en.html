Root ג־י־ס — recruiting & mobilizing

Roots Atlas 241 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ג י ס · the recruiting root

ג י ס
g · y · s
everything to do with mustering people or resources for a cause
3 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

This root is built on the noun גַּיִס, an army corps — a body of troops gathered for a purpose. From that image of mustering comes the whole family: to recruit others (לְגַיֵּס) and to enlist yourself (לְהִתְגַּיֵּס). In modern Israel the active sense has widened far beyond the army: you גַּיֵּס money, volunteers, a whole team. The root lives almost entirely in two binyanim plus their shared action-noun. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְגַיֵּסlegayes
intensive / specialized active
to recruit, to enrol, to raise (funds)
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְגַּיֵּסlehitgayes
reflexive / reciprocal — to / on oneself
to enlist, to join up

The pi'el / hitpa'el pair is the whole story: לְגַיֵּס is doing it to others (you recruit them), לְהִתְגַּיֵּס is doing it to yourself (you sign up). There is no everyday pa'al here — the root works through the intensive pi'el. The matching passive (being recruited) leans on the hitpa'el reflexive rather than a separate pu'al.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
