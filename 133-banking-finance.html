Israeli banking is concentrated: 5 banks (Hapoalim, Leumi, Mizrahi-Tefahot, Discount, FIBI) hold ~95% of the market. Customer service is famously poor; fees are famously high. The system is digital but slow to change. Lesson 17 covered basics; this one goes deeper into investment, credit cards, mortgages, pensions — what you need for full financial life.

Cultural note — Walking into a Hapoalim or Leumi branch is an Israeli rite of passage and often a comedy of bureaucratic delay. You\'ll need an "ת"ז" (ID), patience, and ideally to "do everything online instead." Apps: Pepper (Bank Leumi digital subsidiary, no fees), Bit (P2P payments — verb: "תַּעֲשֶׂה לִי בִּיט" — "send me a bit"). Salaries are paid by direct deposit on the 28-30th of each month.

Bit / Pepper Phrases — Daily Mobile Banking

Israelis pay each other constantly via Bit (Bank Hapoalim) or PayBox. The verb "to bit" is now standard Hebrew. You\'ll hear these phrases at every restaurant, shared apartment, and Friday lunch.
