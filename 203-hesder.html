Hesder — Yeshiva and Army Combined

Lesson 203 · יְשִׁיבוֹת הֶסְדֵּר
Home203

"Hesder" means "arrangement" — a 5-year religious-Zionist program combining yeshiva learning with abbreviated military service (~17-19 months instead of 32-36). About 70 hesder yeshivot enroll roughly 10,000 students. Founded 1953 by Rav Yehuda Amital after the IDF asked yeshiva graduates to choose between Torah and Tzahal — Amital said both. Distinct from haredi yeshivot (full draft exemption) and from full IDF service.

Cultural note — Hesder soldiers serve almost exclusively in elite combat units: Kfir, Givati, Golani, Tzanchanim, Sayeret Givati, Sayeret Tzanchanim. Despite shorter service, casualty rates are higher than average — a hesder soldier is more likely to be in active combat than a regular conscript. The motto "with one hand the sword, with the other the Talmud" is taken literally. When you meet someone with a "kippa srugah" who served in Givati, ~80% chance they did hesder.
