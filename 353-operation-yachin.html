Operation Yachin & Moroccan Aliyah Waves

Lesson 353 · מִבְצַע יָכִין וְעֲלִיַּת מָרוֹקוֹ
Home353

Between 1948 and 1971, roughly 250,000 Moroccan Jews left for Israel — the largest single ethnic exodus of any Jewish community to the Jewish state. The story has three acts. Act one: the messy first wave during Israel's war years (1948-1956), when 30,000 olim crossed under uncertain status. Act two: the freeze, when independent Morocco closed emigration in 1956. Act three: Mivtza Yachin (1961-1964), a covert Mossad operation that smuggled 80,000 Jews out via Spain and Gibraltar after King Hassan II accepted $250 per head in tacit ransom. The Egoz disaster of January 1961 — 44 olim drowned when their boat sank — was the catalyst that forced the negotiation. Settlement was harsh: davka the Sephardim were sent to development towns in the periphery (Dimona, Yeruham, Sderot, Kiryat Shmona) while Ashkenazi olim got the center. The Wadi Salib riots of 1959, the DDT spraying at the ports, the ma\'abarot tents — this is the trauma the Mizrahi memory still carries.

Cultural note — When you arrive in Israel and meet a Moroccan family in Dimona, Beersheva, or Sderot, you are meeting the descendants of this story. Their grandparents did not "come home" the way the Zionist narrative tells it. They were sprayed with DDT at Haifa port, bussed at night to a tent camp in the desert, told their language and music were primitive, and watched their children be channeled into vocational tracks while Polish kids went to university. The Wadi Salib riots in Haifa (July 1959) were the first organized Mizrahi protest. The Black Panthers (Israeli, 1971) were the second. Knowing this history is how you avoid the Ashkenazi-centric blind spot most olim arrive with.
