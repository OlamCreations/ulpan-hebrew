Beit Sefer Yesodi — Elementary School

Lesson 252 · בֵּית סֵפֶר יְסוֹדִי
Home252

Beit sefer yesodi covers grades 1-6 (alef through vav), ages 6-12. The school day typically runs 8am-1:30pm, with longer hours in afternoon programs (tzaharon). Subjects include Hebrew (ivrit), arithmetic (mathematica), English starting in grade 4, Tanach, Israel knowledge (yedi\'at ha-aretz), science (mada), gym (chinuch gufani), art (omanut), and music. School streams: mamlachti (secular), mamlachti dati (religious), Arab schools, and haredi (private). Olim register their children via the iriya (city hall) and Misrad HaChinuch (Ministry of Education).

Cultural note — Israeli elementary school is often described as more relaxed than American or European counterparts: fewer hours, less homework, and much more social play. Olim children typically receive 30-60 hours per year of taisha (Hebrew tutoring) directly at school. Parent involvement is high — most schools run a weekly vaad horim (parent committee). Lunch: most kids bring a sandwich from home; some afternoon programs serve a hot meal. Recess (hafsaka) happens every 45 minutes, lasting 15-30 minutes between classes. Class size is typically 25-35 students.
