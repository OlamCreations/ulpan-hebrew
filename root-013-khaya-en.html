Root ח־י־ה: life

Roots Atlas 13 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ח י ה · the life root
Home ח י ה Morphology
ח י ה
ch · y · h
everything to do with living
10 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: being alive. The same letters give you the verb to live, the adjective alive, the noun life itself, and a whole chain of words about vitality, revival, and what keeps a person going. The third letter ה is weak, so it bends in the vowels, but the meaning never moves. None of these words is memorized separately. They are the same root seen from different angles. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִחְיוֹתlichyot
simple active, the plain action
to live, to be alive
Pi'el
לְחַיֵּהַּlechayeah
intensive / specialized active
to vitalize, to keep alive (let live)
Hif'il
לְהַחֲיוֹתlehachayot
causative, make someone do it
to revive, to resuscitate (bring back to life)

Three binyanim are lexicalized, and they line up neatly: pa'al is simply to live, hif'il is causative, to make live again, to revive. The passive partners nif'al, pu'al and huf'al are not lexicalized for this root, and there is no everyday hitpa'el. That is normal: not every root fills all seven moulds.

Words born from the root
