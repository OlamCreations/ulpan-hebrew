French Jews — Aliyah from France

Lesson 272 · יְהוּדִים צָרְפָתִים
Home272

France has Europe's largest Jewish community: about 440,000 — the biggest after the United States and Israel. The community is mostly Sephardi-Mizrahi, descended from Moroccan, Tunisian, and Algerian Jews who immigrated to France between 1956 and 1965. Today they cluster in Paris (~80% of the community), Marseille, Lyon, Nice, and Strasbourg. French aliyah surged after 2014 in response to antisemitic attacks, peaking around 7,000/year in 2015, with total French olim in Israel now exceeding 150,000. Hotspots: Netanya (the French capital), Ashdod (Mizrahi-French), Bat Yam (working-class), Tel Aviv (professionals), Jerusalem (religious).

Cultural note — Netanya is the "Tel Aviv-on-the-Mediterranean for the French" — boulangeries on every corner, French-language signage, French-speaking medical clinics, and a beachfront promenade that feels like Nice with Hebrew. The 2015 spike followed the Hyper Cacher attack in Paris and a wider wave of antisemitic violence. French Israelis often retain dual citizenship and travel back and forth between Israel and France monthly. The cultural blend is distinctive: Mediterranean kosher cuisine, café culture, and Mizrahi-Sephardi rituals coexist with Parisian habits. French-speaking Israeli media include i24NEWS (founded by French Jews) and the daily Hayom in French. Nefesh B\'Nefesh expanded its operations to France around 2014 to handle the surge.
