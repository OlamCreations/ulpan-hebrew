Formal Letters — Writing in Register

Lesson 293 · מִכְתָּבִים פוֹרְמָלִיִּים
Home293

Hebrew formal correspondence has a distinct register — greetings, sign-offs, sentence structure, and vocabulary all shift from spoken Hebrew. Standard openings: "לכבוד" (lichvod, "to the honorable"), "שלום רב" (shalom rav, "warm greetings"). Standard closings: "בכבוד רב" (b\'kavod rav, "with great respect"), "בברכה" (bivracha, "with blessing"). At B2 you can write a complaint letter, a formal request, a CV cover letter without help.

Cultural note — Hebrew formal letters use longer constructions than spoken Hebrew: future-tense polite requests ("אבקש שתשלח" instead of "תשלח לי"), passive voice for gravity, full noun phrases instead of pronouns. Email is acceptable for most formal contexts; physical letters reserved for legal matters. Address the addressee by full title — "מר סמית" (Mr. Smith), "ד"ר כהן" (Dr. Cohen), "פרופ\' לוי" (Prof. Levi).
