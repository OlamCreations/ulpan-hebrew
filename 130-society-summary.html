Israeli Society — The Cleavages

Lesson 130 · הַשְּׁבָרִים בַּחֶבְרָה
Home130

Closing Block 13 with a synthesis: the "fault lines" (שְׁבָרִים) that organize Israeli social life and politics. There are roughly six: Jewish/Arab, Religious/Secular, Ashkenazi/Mizrahi, Right/Left, Native-born/Immigrant, Tel Aviv/Periphery. Most Israelis sit on multiple axes at once. Knowing them lets you read any social interaction — and any political column — within seconds.

Cultural note — There\'s a famous Israeli sociologist quote: "Israel is not a melting pot. It\'s a cluster of co-located tribes." Rivlin\'s 2015 "Four Tribes" speech identified secular, national-religious, charedi, and Arab as the four growing demographics. Knowing where someone sits on these axes — or refuses to sit — predicts almost everything about their politics.
