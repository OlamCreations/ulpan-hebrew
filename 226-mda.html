Magen David Adom — Israel's EMS

Lesson 226 · מָגֵן דָּוִד אָדוֹם
Home226

Magen David Adom (MDA, "Red Shield of David") is Israel's national emergency medical service — the equivalent of US 911 EMS or French SAMU. The number is 101, free from any phone, anywhere in the country. Founded in 1930, MDA operates with ~25,000 volunteers and ~3,000 paid staff, running ambulances, mobile ICU units (Natan), motorbike paramedics, and the national blood bank. Post-October 7, average response time to terror attacks is 7 minutes nationwide.

Cultural note — Calling 101 typically connects you with a Hebrew-speaking operator first; English speakers can request a transfer with "Speak English please." Provide: location (rehov + mispar bayit), nature of emergency, age and condition of the patient. MDA dispatches a paramedic-staffed ambulance for cardiac and severe trauma, a basic ambulance for transport. Standard reporting terms include "pa\'amati" (vital signs) and "hodaah dachufa" (urgent message). Israelis call 101 without hesitation, even for moderate emergencies — the system is built for volume, not for triage anxiety.
