פ ת ח
p · t · ch
everything to do with opening
9 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

One idea opens this whole family: opening. Open a door and that is pa'al; the gap you open is an entrance; the tool that opens locks is a key; and when you open up a raw idea over time, you develop it. That last twist is the gift of this root: in Hebrew, development is literally an unfolding, an opening-out. Each word below is a different way of opening, verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִפְתֹּחַliftoach
simple active, the plain action
to open, to unlock; to switch on
Nif'al
לְהִפָּתֵחַlehipateach
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be opened; to begin, to commence
Pi'el
לְפַתֵּחַlefateach
intensive / specialized active
to develop (something); to invent

Watch the binyan split the meaning in two: pa'al opens a physical thing, while pi'el לְפַתֵּחַ takes the same opening into the abstract, to develop, to bring out the potential. The nif'al is the passive ("to be opened") and, beautifully, also "to begin": an event opens.

Words born from the root
