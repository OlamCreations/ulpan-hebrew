Ne'emanei Eretz Yisrael — Loyalists of the Land

Lesson 209 · נֶאֱמָנֵי אֶרֶץ יִשְׂרָאֵל
Home209

Ne'emanei Eretz Yisrael ("Loyalists of the Land of Israel") is the umbrella term for hardline religious-Zionist activists committed to Israeli sovereignty over the entire historic Land. The movement crystallized after the Camp David Accords (1978) and intensified after Oslo (1993) and the Disengagement (2005). It is distinct from mainstream religious Zionism, more uncompromising on territorial integrity, sometimes clashing with Israeli state authorities. Includes "Hill Youth" (no'ar ha-gva'ot), Gush Emunim heirs, and rabbinical leadership (Rav Tzvi Tau, Rav Shlomo Aviner, Rav Lior).

Cultural note — The disengagement from Gaza in August 2005 was the trauma that hardened this movement. Soldiers carrying out evictions of Jewish residents were called "machtarot" (the destroyers); the residents were "mitnachluyim" being torn from "kedusha." Many religious-Zionist soldiers serve in IDF without conflict; some refuse orders involving evictions. This boundary, "do you obey rabbi or commander", is one of religious Zionism's deepest unresolved tensions. The state often wins, the wound stays.
