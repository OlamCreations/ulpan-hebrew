Root נ־ג־ן: playing music

Roots Atlas 68 · שֹׁרֶשׁ נ ג ן · the music-playing root
Home נ ג ן Morphology
נ ג ן
n · g · n
everything to do with playing an instrument and the melody it makes
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root for the whole world of instrumental music. To play an instrument, the player who does it, the melody that results, and the very act of playing all live in נ-ג-ן. Notice there is no plain pa'al here: the action runs entirely through the intensive pi'el לְנַגֵּן, which fits a deliberate, sustained activity like making music. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְנַגֵּןlenagen
intensive / specialized active
to play (a musical instrument)
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְנַגֵּןlehitnagen
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to be played, to play itself (of music)

This root skips the plain pa'al entirely: "to play" is the intensive pi'el לְנַגֵּן, because making music is a deliberate, repeated act. Its reflexive partner hitpa'el לְהִתְנַגֵּן means the music "plays itself", what comes out of the speakers. The remaining binyanim are not lexicalized for this root.

Words born from the root
