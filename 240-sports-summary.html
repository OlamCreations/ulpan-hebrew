Block 24 closes the sports & leisure section. Israeli weekend leisure runs on a few rails: football derbies that double as political tribes, basketball EuroLeague nights, beach matkot all summer, hiking the Shvil, gym workouts, scuba diving in Eilat, weekend parks with the family, and the slow Shabbat rhythm that bookends it all. The 10 lessons of the block covered football (Hapoel/Maccabi/Beitar), basketball (Maccabi TLV + Hapoel Jerusalem), beach culture and matkot, the Israel National Trail, gyms, Eilat diving, Hapoel Jerusalem deep dive, parks, and the weekend rhythm. This summary consolidates the 10 essential terms, the key institutions, the main events, and the ideological pairs that structure how Israelis talk about leisure.

Cultural note — Israeli sports culture is intensely tribal: your team is your political and cultural marker, not just an entertainment preference. Beach matkot is the universal sound of summer — a constant tok-tok along every coastline from Nahariya to Eilat. The Shvil Yisrael is the country\'s hiking pilgrimage, often done section by section over years. Most Israelis combine three loyalties: a sports team, a beach, and a weekend family routine. Knowing this vocabulary unlocks coffee-shop conversations, water-cooler chats, Friday lunches, and the small talk before any meeting. After Block 24 you can hold your own on Hapoel vs Maccabi, plan a beach day, book a dive course in Eilat, or ask someone what they\'re doing this weekend — basic social fluency for any oleh.
