Root י־ר־ד — descending

Roots Atlas 81 · שֹׁרֶשׁ י ר ד · the descending root
Home י ר ד Morphology
י ר ד
y · r · d
everything to do with going down — descending, lowering, declining
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

The core is a single direction: down. The pa'al לָרֶדֶת is to descend, to go down; the hif'il לְהוֹרִיד is its causative, to bring something down, to lower, and in the computer age, to download. From the same root come the noun descent / decline (יְרִידָה) and the adjective low-grade, inferior (יָרוּד), what has come down in quality. Two of these carry a heavy second meaning in Israeli life: a yerida is also emigration from Israel, the mirror of aliyah (going up). This is a first-yod (pe-yod) root, so the י often drops or shifts to a vowel in conjugation. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לָרֶדֶתlaredet
simple active — the plain action
to descend, to go down; to emigrate from Israel
Hif'il
לְהוֹרִידlehorid
causative — make someone do it
to lower, to bring down, to reduce; to download

The pa'al / hif'il pair is the classic causative contrast: laredet is to go down yourself, lehorid is to bring something else down. In the pa'al the initial י vanishes (לָרֶדֶת); in the hif'il it surfaces as the וֹ vowel (לְהוֹרִיד), the regular behaviour of a first-yod root. "Download" is simply the newest use of "bring it down".

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
