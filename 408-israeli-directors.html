Major Israeli Film Directors

Lesson 408 · בַּמַּאִים יִשְׂרְאֵלִים
Home408

Israeli cinema has a strange shape. It is small (a country of nine million), it lives between Europe (festival circuit) and the Middle East (its subject matter), and it is unusually fearless about politics. Its directors keep arriving at three obsessions: the army (every Israeli has served), the family (Mizrahi, Ashkenazi, religious, secular — never neutral), and the territory (occupation, borders, who belongs where). This lesson maps the field — from Amos Gitai's prolific decades to the new women directors reshaping the canon.

Cultural note — The Israeli Film Fund and the Cinema Project of the Israel Lottery (Mifal HaPais) finance most domestic films. Cannes, Venice, Berlin recognize Israeli cinema disproportionately for its size — Foxtrot, Lebanon, Footnote, Gett, Waltz with Bashir all won major prizes. The Sapir Prize (Israeli Oscar) is the home equivalent. Tel Aviv Cinematheque + Jerusalem Cinematheque are the cultural temples — every serious Israeli film passes through them.
