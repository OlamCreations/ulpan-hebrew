Lesson 398 · יְהוּדֵי אָמֶרִיקָה — עָרֵי הַיַּהֲדוּת
Home398

American Jewry is a constellation of neighborhoods, not a single block. ~7.5M Jews across the US, but density is what matters: half of them live within 90 minutes of New York, and the rest cluster in five or six metros where you can buy challah, find a minyan, send kids to day school, and live a recognizably Jewish week without explanation. This lesson maps that geography in Hebrew — the names Israelis use when they talk about American Jewish life, from Borough Park to Pico-Robertson.

Cultural note — When Israelis ask an American oleh "מֵאֵיפֹה אַתָּה?" (where are you from?), the answer "New York" or "LA" is a starting point, not a destination. The follow-up is always the neighborhood: Crown Heights, Teaneck, Pico, Brookline. Each name carries a profile — denomination, Hebrew level, Zionism, kashrut standard, even politics. Knowing the map is knowing the person. Borough Park ≠ Park Slope, even though both are Brooklyn. Five Towns ≠ Westchester, even though both are commute-distance from Manhattan. The geography IS the sociology.
