Religion & State — The Status Quo

Lesson 128 · דָּת וּמְדִינָה
Home128

Israel is officially "Jewish and democratic" — never explicitly defined how to balance the two. The "Status Quo" agreement (Ben-Gurion, 1947, before Independence) set 4 compromises: Shabbat as the official rest day, kashrut in state institutions, Orthodox monopoly on personal status, separate religious schools. Every Israeli political crisis ultimately renegotiates this.

Cultural note — In Israel, you cannot get civilly married. Marriage and divorce go through religious courts (Jewish, Muslim, Christian, Druze). Jews who want to bypass this fly to Cyprus or Las Vegas — the foreign marriage is then recognized. ~30% of secular couples now choose this path. The phrase "טַסְנוּ לְקַפְרִיסִין" ("we flew to Cyprus") is shorthand for civilly married.
