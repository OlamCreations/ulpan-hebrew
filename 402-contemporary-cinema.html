Contemporary Israeli Cinema

Lesson 402 · קוֹלְנוֹעַ יִשְׂרְאֵלִי בֶּן זְמַנֵּנוּ
Home402

From the late 1990s onward, Israeli cinema entered a wave of international recognition unprecedented in its history. Ten Oscar nominations for Best Foreign Language Film between 1965 and 2024, zero wins, but a near-permanent presence on the shortlist. Directors like Joseph Cedar, Ari Folman, Eytan Fox, Eran Riklis, Amos Gitai, Samuel Maoz brought Israeli stories — war, occupation, ultra-Orthodox communities, mixed neighborhoods, gay love, family memory — to Cannes, Venice, the Berlinale, and the Academy. This lesson covers the films, the directors, the industry vocabulary in Hebrew.

Cultural note — Israel\'s Oscar shortlist record (10 nominations) puts it among the most-nominated countries per capita in cinema history. The "zero wins" is famous in Israel itself, sometimes joked about, sometimes lamented. What it actually shows: a small country (9.5M people) producing films that consistently meet the highest international standard. The Israeli Film Fund (Keren ha-Kolnoa) and the Rabinovich Foundation finance most of these productions on budgets a fraction of European or American counterparts.
