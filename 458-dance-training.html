Israeli dance training runs through three main academies: the Telma Yellin High School of the Arts (Givatayim), the Bat-Dor Studios (TLV), and the Jerusalem Academy of Music and Dance. Above them sit the company training programmes (Batsheva Ensemble, KCDC International Dance Village). Add the after-school children\'s schools (Habama Studios, regional gan-machol classes), and you have the pipeline that produces the dancers seen at Suzanne Dellal. This lesson covers the schools, the audition process, the IDF dance soldiers programme, and what a serious training day looks like.

Cultural note — Telma Yellin is to Israeli dance what the Lycée Henri-IV is to French academia: get in at age 14, train 8 hours a day for 4 years, graduate ready for any company audition. Many Batsheva and KCDC dancers came through Telma Yellin or its smaller cousins (Hatzav, Reut). Then comes the IDF "outstanding artists" programme — gifted dancers can substitute regular military service for continuing performance commitments tied to public outreach.
