Judaica — the visual culture of Jewish ritual objects — runs from the silver Hanukkiyah on a Friday-night table to the museum-grade ketubah scrolls of Renaissance Italy. Yemenite filigree, Iraqi engraving, Moroccan repoussé, Eastern European folk paper-cut, and the modern Israeli Stieglitz silver studio all live in the same field. This lesson covers the major object types, the materials, the leading studios, and the shopping vocabulary you need at Bezalel Street or in the Old City.

Cultural note — A wedding gift in Israel is often a piece of Judaica: a kiddush cup, a salt-and-pepper Shabbat set, a mezuzah for the new home. Knowing the difference between a 925 silver hallmark and silver-plate is real consumer literacy here. The Stieglitz family workshop in Jaffa is the modern Israeli silver gold-standard; their pieces sit in major museum collections.
