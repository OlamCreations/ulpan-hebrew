Prescriptions — Reading and Filling

Lesson 224 · מִרְשָׁמִים
Home224

Israeli prescriptions (mirsham) are mostly digital — issued by your doctor through the kupa system, automatically synced to participating pharmacies. Pick up at any kupa-affiliated pharmacy or partnered chain (Super-Pharm, Be Pharm). Generic medications are heavily subsidized; brand-name medications often require copay. The "Sal HaBriut" (basic medications basket) is updated annually by the Ministry of Health — what's in vs. out determines coverage.

Cultural note — The "Sal HaBriut update" each January is national news in Israel; patient advocacy groups lobby for new drugs to be included, and decisions can mean life-or-death for chronic conditions. As an oleh, your initial 6-12 months of subsidized coverage covers most basic medications, but specialty drugs (cancer, rare diseases) may need supplemental insurance. Israeli pharmacists (rokeach) are highly trained; they can answer detailed questions about interactions, dosage, alternatives.
