French Jewry — pre-aliyah roots

Lesson 311 · יַהֲדוּת צָרְפַת לִפְנֵי הָעֲלִיָּה
Home311

Before you make aliyah, know where you come from. French Jewry is one of the oldest continuous Jewish communities in Europe — Rashi was teaching in Troyes in the 11th century, Napoleon emancipated them in 1791, and Dreyfus split the country in two. The post-1956 wave from North Africa rebuilt the demographic mass after the Shoah. Roughly 500K Jews lived in France at the 2000s peak, the third-largest community in the world. Understanding this heritage is part of becoming an Israeli — your story is woven into theirs.

Cultural note — French Jewry is split between Ashkenazim (descendants of Rashi, Alsace, post-WWII Polish/Lithuanian survivors) and Sephardim (Maghreb arrivals 1956-67, today the demographic majority at ~70%). The Consistoire administers Ashkenazi religious life, the CRIF speaks politically for both. The Pletzl in the Marais (4th arrondissement) is the historical Yiddish quarter; Sarcelles, Créteil, Strasbourg and Marseille are today\'s living centers. When you arrive in Israel, "tzarfati" (French) on a name tag carries this entire history.
