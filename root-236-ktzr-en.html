Root ק־צ־ר: shortness & harvest

ק צ ר
k · tz · r
everything to do with being short and with reaping
9 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One blade cuts two ways. The core of ק־צ־ר is a cut that makes something shorter. Cut the standing grain and you harvest; cut a text and you shorten it. So the same root gives the reaper and the harvest on one side, and shortness, abbreviation, and a summary on the other, even the electrical "short-circuit". Every word below grows from that single act of cutting short. Each is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִקְצֹרliktzor
simple active, the plain action
to harvest, to reap
Pi'el
לְקַצֵּרlekatzer
intensive / specialized active
to shorten

The split between the two binyanim mirrors the split of meaning: the plain pa'al לִקְצֹר keeps the physical, agricultural sense (reap the grain), while the intensive pi'el לְקַצֵּר carries the figurative one (shorten a text, a route, a trip). Same root, two jobs.

Words born from the root
