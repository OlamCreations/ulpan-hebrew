Israeli Stage Actors

Lesson 415 · שַׂחְקָנֵי בָּמָה יִשְׂרְאֵלִיִּים
Home415

Israeli theatre is a small world with deep roots. The stage trained the screen — almost every great Israeli film actor comes through Habima, Cameri, Beit Lessin, or one of three theatre schools (Beit Tzvi in Ramat Gan, Nissan Native in Tel Aviv, Sam Spiegel theatre stream). This lesson maps the stage genealogy from the Habima founders who arrived from Moscow in 1928 to the contemporary actors working today in Tel Aviv. If you want to understand Israeli culture, learn the names that get clapped at curtain call.

Cultural note — Habima (הַבִּימָה, "the stage") is the National Theatre, founded in Moscow 1917 by Stanislavsky students, moved to Palestine 1928. Cameri (הַקַּאמֶרִי) was founded 1944 in Tel Aviv as a younger, more vernacular alternative — colloquial Hebrew instead of Habima\'s biblical declamation. The two theatres define the Israeli stage. Beit Tzvi (1955), Nissan Native (1963), and the Sam Spiegel school (newer) train almost everyone who works.
