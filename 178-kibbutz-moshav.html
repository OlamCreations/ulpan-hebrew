Kibbutz & Moshav — Israeli Cooperative Agriculture

Lesson 178 · קִיבּוּץ וּמוֹשָׁב
Home178

The kibbutz (collective) and moshav (cooperative) are Israeli inventions — communal forms of rural settlement that built much of the country and still produce 40% of agricultural output despite hosting only 5% of the population. Knowing the structures, vocabulary, and decision-making processes is essential for working in Israeli agriculture, even more so post-aliyah if you join one as a hired worker, candidate member, or research partner.

Cultural note — The classical kibbutz (collective ownership, no private property, communal dining) has largely privatized since the 1990s. Today most kibbutzim are "renewed kibbutzim" (kibbutz mitchadesh) — partial privatization, salaries, but still cooperative governance. Moshavim were always more individual: family-owned plots, joint marketing. Both remain backbones of Negev/Galilee agriculture and have specific Hebrew vocabulary you\'ll need.
