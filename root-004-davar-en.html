Root ד־ב־ר: speaking, word, thing

Roots Atlas 04 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ד ב ר · the speaking root
Home ד ב ר Morphology
ד ב ר
d · b · r
everything to do with speaking, the word, and the thing
12 words · 1 root · 4 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one of Hebrew's deepest ideas. ד־ב־ר is speaking, but its central noun, דָּבָר, means both "word" and "thing". In this root a word and a thing are the same: to say something is to make it real. Pour the letters into a verb mould and you get an act of speech; into a noun mould and you get the speaker, the speech, a commandment, even the desert where one place could be reached only by a spoken word. None of the words below are memorized separately. They are the same three letters seen from different angles. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Nif'al
לְהִדָּבֵרlehidaver
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to agree, to come to an agreement
Pi'el
לְדַבֵּרledaber
intensive / specialized active
to speak, to talk
Hif'il
לְהַדְבִּירlehadbir
causative, make someone do it
to vanquish, to subdue (cause to submit)
Hitpa'el
לְהִדַּבֵּרlehidaber
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to discuss, to talk things through together

The everyday verb "to speak" is the pi'el דִּבֵּר, not a pa'al, the simple pa'al is not lexicalized for this root. Notice the pair: nif'al (להידבר, to reach agreement) and hitpa'el (להידבר, to talk it through) share almost the same form and the reciprocal idea of two parties exchanging words. The hif'il drifts to a harder sense, to subdue, the way a word can also command.
