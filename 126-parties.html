Parties & Political Camps

Lesson 126 · מִפְלָגוֹת
Home126

Israeli parties come and go — Likud and Labor are the only ones that have lasted decades. Most parties last 1-3 elections, then merge, split, or disappear. The deeper structure is camps (גּוּשִׁים): right-religious, center-secular, Arab, far-left. Once you understand the camps, individual party names matter less — they\'re just temporary vehicles for stable constituencies.

Cultural note — The split between "Bibi" and "Anti-Bibi" has been the dominant axis of Israeli politics since ~2015 — sometimes overriding traditional left/right. Inside the right camp, the major fault line is between secular Likud and religious-Zionist Otzma. Inside the center, between Yair Lapid\'s technocrats and Benny Gantz\'s ex-generals.
