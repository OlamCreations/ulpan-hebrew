Root ר־ג־ל: the foot

Roots Atlas 77 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ר ג ל · the foot root
Home ר ג ל Morphology
ר ג ל
r · g · l
everything to do with the foot, the path it treads, and the habits it wears in
7 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

One concrete thing sits at the bottom: the foot (רֶגֶל). Watch what grows from it. A foot that walks the same path again and again wears a groove, and that groove is a habit (הֶרְגֵּל); the person who has worn it in is used to it (רָגִיל), which is also why רָגִיל means "usual". A foot that follows someone secretly is spying (לְרַגֵּל). The same three letters carry you from a body part to a routine to an act of surveillance, with no separate memorization. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְרַגֵּלleragel
intensive / specialized active
to spy, to shadow, to tail
Hif'il
לְהַרְגִּילlehargil
causative, make someone do it
to habituate, to accustom (someone to)
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְרַגֵּלlehitragel
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to get used to, to become accustomed

Notice the hif'il / hitpa'el pair: lehargil is causative (I accustom you), lehitragel is its reflexive (I accustom myself, i.e. I get used to it). The simple pa'al "to walk on foot" is archaic and not in everyday use; modern Hebrew expresses walking with other verbs, so the living verbs here are the habit-and-spying ones.
