Block 27 closes the real estate and housing section. Israeli housing follows a clear sequence: search, then contract, then mortgage, then vaad bayit, then arnona, then commute via highways or rail. The lessons covered apartment search on Yad2, rental contracts, mashkanta, the Tel Aviv vs periphery split, neighborhoods, gentrifikatzia, arnona, vaad bayit, and the Kvish 6 commute. After Block 27, you can navigate any apartment search, sign a contract, get a mortgage, pay arnona, attend a vaad meeting, and choose between premium toll routes and free alternatives. This summary consolidates the 10 essential terms, the key institutions, and the key concepts.

Cultural note — Real estate vocabulary is critical because most olim's first major life decision is where to live. The Tel Aviv premium is real but suffocating; the periphery is affordable but isolating; Modi'in and Beit Shemesh balance both. Knowing the lexicon means signing contracts confidently, applying for mortgages directly, and navigating disputes. After Block 27, you can read Yad2 listings fluently, understand mortgage clauses, and contribute meaningfully at a vaad bayit meeting — full housing fluency in 10 lessons.
