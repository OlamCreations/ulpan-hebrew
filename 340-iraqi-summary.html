Block 34 Summary — Iraqi/Babylonian Jewry

Lesson 340 · סִכּוּם יְהוּדֵי בָּבֶל וְעִירָאק
Home340

Closing Block 34 with a synthesis of the longest continuous Jewish exile community — 2,500 years from the Babylonian Galut to the airlift of 1950-52. Lessons 331-339 covered the historical anchors (586 BCE → 1941), the Talmud Bavli that emerged from Sura and Pumbedita, the spiritual giants from Rav Ashi to Ben Ish Hai, the trauma of the Farhud, the dramatic exodus of Operation Ezra and Nehemiah, the food and music and language that survived the rupture, and the modern Israeli figures who carry the legacy. This lesson consolidates it all.

Cultural note — Iraqi Jewry is the foundational diaspora. Every Ashkenazi prayer book, every halakhic ruling, every yeshiva structure traces back to Sura and Pumbedita. When you meet an Israeli with the family name Sassoon, Iraqi, Bavli, Mizrahi, or hear a great-grandparent who spoke Judeo-Iraqi qeltu Arabic, you are touching 2,500 years of unbroken transmission. Or Yehuda outside Tel Aviv is the heritage center. Dudu Tassa is the cultural revival. Sami Michael is the literary memory. The Farhud is the wound. Operation Ezra and Nehemiah is the rescue.
