Wix — Drag-and-Drop Empire from Tel Aviv

Lesson 214 · וִיקְס
Home214

Wix.com was founded in 2006 by Avishai Abrahami (CEO), his brother Nadav Abrahami, and Giora Kaplan. The product is a drag-and-drop website builder on a freemium model, listed on Nasdaq in 2013 in a $600M IPO. Today Wix has roughly 270M users globally, around $1.5B in annual revenue, and ~5500 employees (~3500 of them in Israel). Headquarters sit in Tel Aviv near the harbor — Wix is the textbook case of an Israeli SaaS company scaling globally, with most of its users being American small businesses, not Israelis.

Cultural note — Wix represents the "global SaaS" path versus the more common Israeli "exit-and-restart" path: Wix never sold, kept growing publicly, and became one of Israel\'s largest tech employers. The Tel Aviv HQ is a campus near Tel Aviv Port (Namel) and is a destination tour stop for visiting tech delegations. Avishai Abrahami remains CEO 18+ years after founding, which is unusual in Israeli tech. The product line spans the website builder, e-commerce, Wix Restaurants, and CRM. Marketing strategy famously includes Super Bowl ads featuring celebrities (Christoph Waltz, Heidi Klum, Helen Mirren) — almost unheard of for an Israeli company.
