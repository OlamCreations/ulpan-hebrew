Latin American Jews — Aliyah from Argentina, Brazil, Mexico

Lesson 274 · יְהוּדִים מֵאָמֵרִיקָה הַלָטִינִית
Home274

Latin American Jewish community: ~400,000 across Argentina (~180K — largest), Brazil (~92K), Mexico (~40K), Venezuela (declining), Chile, Uruguay, Colombia. Mostly Ashkenazi (descended from European immigrants 1880-1939) with a Sephardi minority. Aliyah from LatAm runs ~1,500-3,000/year, surging during regional crises (Argentina 2001 financial collapse, Venezuela post-2010, Brazil 2014+). Hotspots in Israel: Tel Aviv, Hod HaSharon, Ra\'anana, Kfar Saba (Argentinian Spanish), Beit Shemesh.

Cultural note — Argentinian olim are the largest Latin American group, bringing tango, mate culture, asado (Argentine BBQ), and Buenos Aires-style cafe culture. Brazilian olim cluster in north Tel Aviv + Hod HaSharon. Mexican olim often work in Tel Aviv tech (engineers from UNAM). The "Avenida Argentina" in Hod HaSharon is a real local shorthand. LatAm olim integrate easily socially (warm Mediterranean vibe is similar) but often hit the Hebrew language barrier longer than Anglos. Recent trend: Venezuelan and Argentine economic refugees increase the flow.
