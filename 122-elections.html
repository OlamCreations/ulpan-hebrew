Elections — How Israelis Vote

Lesson 122 · בְּחִירוֹת
Home122

Israelis vote for a party, not a candidate. You drop a single paper slip with two Hebrew letters into an envelope, into a box. That slip is your vote for an entire ranked list. Election Day is a national holiday — schools and most workplaces closed. Israel has had 5 elections in 4 years (2019-2022) — chronic instability is part of the system. This lesson covers the actual mechanics.

Cultural note — On election night, Israelis cluster around TVs at 10 PM for the "מִדְגָּם" (exit poll). Channels 11, 12, and 13 each release one. The first 1-2 minutes of polling night dictate the next morning\'s headlines, often wrongly. Final official results take 2-3 days because IDF soldiers and embassy ballots are counted last.
