Arab & Druze Cuisine

Lesson 144 · מִטְבָּח עֲרָבִי וְדְרוּזִי
Home144

Galilee Arab kitchens and Druze villages on Mount Carmel produce some of the finest food in Israel. Roasted lamb, freshly-baked tabun bread, hand-rolled stuffed grape leaves, knafeh, sahlab. Druze villages — Daliyat al-Karmel, Yirka, Beit Jann — are weekend pilgrimage spots for Israeli foodies. The food crossed into mainstream Israeli cuisine; the Druze hospitality (כָּרוֹת — karot) is unmatched.

Cultural note — A Druze meal is a feast — never less than 15 small dishes (מַזֶּה) before the main. Drinking coffee and offering food are sacred hospitality acts. Knafeh originated in Nablus / the Galilee and is now consumed everywhere — debates about which version is best (Nablusi vs. Akko vs. Daliyat) are constant. Stuffed grape leaves (יָאלָנְגִ\'י / וָרָק עִינַב) are a defining Levantine dish.
