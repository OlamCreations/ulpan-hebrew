Lesson 165 · אוֹכֶל בְּיִידִישׁ
Home165

Ashkenazi food in Israel is its own register — old Bukovina recipes adapted to Tel Aviv kitchens, holiday foods kept alive by grandmothers, deli items rebranded as artisanal. Knowing this vocabulary opens doors at Friday-night tables, kosher delis, and especially the homes of haredim and Russian-Israeli olim.

Cultural note — Block 15 (lessons 141-150) covered Mizrahi-Ashkenazi cuisine generally; this lesson goes deep into Ashkenazi/Yiddish-named dishes. The hierarchy of pity for these foods in Israel: gefilte fish (mocked but loved), kishke (lost art), cholent (universally adored). Note: Mizrahi Israelis often grew up rejecting Ashkenazi food but now love it as nostalgia.
