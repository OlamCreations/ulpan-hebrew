Social Protests — A National Tradition

Lesson 125 · מַחֲאָה חֶבְרָתִית
Home125

Israeli society protests constantly and at scale. The protest is a recurring institution: the Black Panthers (1971, Mizrahi rights), the 400,000 (1982, after Lebanon), the Cottage Cheese Boycott (2011), the J14 tent-city (2011, housing), the 2023 anti-judicial-reform Saturdays, the post-Oct-7 hostage protests. The form repeats: Saturday night, Kaplan Street or HaBima Square, large flags, repeated chants.

Cultural note — "מַחֲאָה" (macha\'a) is the protest noun. Israelis don\'t say "rally" or "demonstration" for everything — they distinguish הַפְגָנָה (planned), מִחְאָה (sustained movement), שְׁבִיתָה (strike), and מַחֲסוֹם (blockade). The phrase "אֲנַחְנוּ הָעָם" — "we are the people" — was the unifying chant of 2023.

Hostage Square — Post-October 7 Protest Phrases

After October 7, 2023, "Hostage Square" (כִּכַּר הַחֲטוּפִים) at the Tel Aviv Museum became the focal point for hostage families and supporters. These phrases echo on every Saturday night.
