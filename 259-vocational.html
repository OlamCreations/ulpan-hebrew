Vocational & Professional — Trades, Tech, Certificates

Lesson 259 · הַכְשָׁרָה מָקְצוֹעִית
Home259

Israeli vocational education is robust: technical high schools (tichon technologi), 2-year colleges (mishaltiyot), professional certifications (machshiv), and tech bootcamps (Hack-IL, Cyberbit, ITC, Coding Academy, App-Sage). Hand-on trades: chashmalim (electricians), chashmalim mam'siyim (industrial electricians), nagarim (carpenters), bnaim (builders), tankaim (mechanics). High-demand certifications: AWS, Microsoft, Israeli IDF Tech specialties post-discharge. Funded options: Misrad HaKlita scholarships for olim, IDF post-discharge programs.

Cultural note — Vocational education is socially less prestigious than university but increasingly respected — Israeli tech industry hires hundreds of bootcamp graduates each year. Anglo olim often skip university and go directly to coding bootcamp or AWS certification. Cyberbit (cybersecurity training, ex-Cyber Reason) is industry-leading. The IDF discharge bonus (4,000-15,000 NIS) often funds first vocational training. Female olim find specific scholarship programs through MASA, ZIONESS, EZRA, plus IDF reservist tracks.
