Notable Bukharan-Caucasian Figures

Lesson 387 · דְּמוּיוֹת בּוּכָרִיוֹת-קַוְקָזִיּוֹת
Home387

Beyond statistics, the Bukharan, Mountain (Juhuro), and Georgian-Jewish communities have produced soldiers, oligarchs, rabbis, artists, and philanthropists whose names you will hear in Israel. Knowing who they are signals respect to the communities you will live alongside in Jerusalem, Or Yehuda, Holon, and Be׳er Sheva. This lesson is the prosopography — the names, the biographies, the Hebrew used to describe their roles.

Cultural note — Each community has its own lions. Bukharans honor Lev Leviev as the philanthropist-king who funded their world network. Mountain Jews rally around German Zakharyaev and the STMEGI foundation. Georgian Jews trace pride through chief rabbis and the Khinkali-eating cultural diplomats. Avigdor Kahalani is universally claimed — his tank battalion held Golan in 1973 and he wears the Ot ha-Mofet. When you cite these names correctly in Hebrew, you stop being a stranger and start being someone who paid attention.
