Root ל־ב־ש: wearing / dressing

Roots Atlas 37 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ל ב ש · the wearing / dressing root
Home ל ב ש Morphology
ל ב ש
l · v · sh
everything to do with clothing and putting it on
6 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three consonants for one daily gesture: putting on a garment. The simple verb is what you do alone in the morning; turn it reflexive and it becomes getting yourself dressed. Then the noun moulds line up the results: the clothing you wear, the act of clothing someone, and the whole outfit. Notice how the same three letters slide from an action to a state (already dressed) to a thing (the attire). Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִלְבֹּשׁlilbosh
simple active, the plain action
to put on (a garment), to wear
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְלַבֵּשׁlehitlabesh
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to get dressed, to dress oneself

The hitpa'el here does exactly what reflexives do: it turns the action back on the doer, so "to put on" becomes "to dress oneself". The causative "to dress someone else" is handled by the hif'il (לְהַלְבִּישׁ), whose action-noun הַלְבָּשָׁה you meet below. Not every binyan is lexicalized for this root, which is normal.

Words born from the root
