Root ג־ז־ר: cutting

ג ז ר
g · z · r
everything to do with cutting and deciding
8 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: cutting. A judge who cuts a case in two issues a verdict; a knife that cuts gives you a piece, or a carrot. The same letters cut a body into shapely sectors, cut a word from another word (derivation), and, in grammar and maths, cut things into clean categories. One physical gesture, branching into law, food, geometry, and language. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִגְזֹרligzor
simple active, the plain action
to cut; to decree, to sentence
Nif'al
לְהִגָּזֵרlehigazer
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be cut off; to be decreed; to be derived

The pa'al holds both senses at once: to cut a material and to cut a decision (a court "cuts" a sentence). The nif'al is its passive, to be cut, and, in grammar, to be derived from another word, since a derived word is one "cut from" its source. The remaining binyanim are not lexicalized for this root.
