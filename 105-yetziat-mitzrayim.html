Yetziat Mitzrayim — The Exodus

Lesson 105 · יְצִיאַת מִצְרַיִם
Home105

The defining narrative of the Jewish people: from slavery in Egypt to freedom at Sinai. The story is retold every Pesach (Passover) at the seder, and referenced in nearly every Israeli political speech, song, and protest. Moshe in the basket, the burning bush, the ten plagues, the night of the firstborn, the splitting of the Yam Suf — every Israeli child learns these in primary school.

Cultural note — "מבית עבדים" ("from the house of slavery") is used in modern Hebrew to describe any escape from oppression. Pesach is the most-observed Jewish holiday, even by chiloni Israelis: the seder night is family-mandatory like Christmas dinner is in much of Europe.

After crossing the Yam Suf, Moshe and the people sing this song. Recited every morning in the daily prayer service. Written in Torah scrolls in a unique brick-like layout.
