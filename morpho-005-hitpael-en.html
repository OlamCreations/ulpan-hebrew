Hitpa'el — the reflexive

Morphology 05 · בִּנְיָן הִתְפַּעֵל
Home M1 M2 M3 M4 M5 M6 M7 M8

The seventh binyan, and the easiest to spot: every form wears the prefix הִתְ (past) or מִתְ (present). Its job is to turn the action back on the subject — you do it to yourself, you do it to each other, or you gradually become something. Got dressed, washed up, got excited, corresponded, joined in. If a verb starts with hit- / mit-, it is almost always here.

Three flavours of "back on the subject" — Reflexive (to oneself): הִתְלַבֵּשׁ got dressed, הִתְרַחֵץ washed up. Reciprocal (each other): הִתְכַּתֵּב corresponded, הִתְנַשֵּׁק kissed. Becoming (gradual change of state): הִתְרַגֵּשׁ got excited, הִתְקַדֵּם advanced. Mould: past הִתְ_ַ_ֵּ_, present מִתְ_ַ_ֵּ_.

Plain hitpa'el — the prefix is the whole story

The one twist: hissing letters swap places — When the root starts with a sibilant (ס שׁ שׂ ז צ), the ת of הִתְ trades places with that letter so it is easier to say. This is metathesis. You will hear st-, sht-, zd-, tzt- at the front. It is regular — learn the four cases and you will never be fooled by them.

Expected *hit-shamesh is hard to say, so שׁ and ת swap → hishtamesh. The root is still ש-מ-ש (serve / use). Reverse the swap in your head and the root reappears.

Decode drill — undo the prefix (and the swap)

Strip הִתְ / מִתְ. If the front hisses (st, sht, zd, tzt), undo the metathesis to find the real root.
