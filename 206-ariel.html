Ariel — City and University

Lesson 206 · אֲרִיאֵל — עִיר וְאוּנִיבֶרְסִיטָה
Home206

Ariel is the largest Israeli urban community in Yesha — about 22,000 residents in central Samaria, founded in 1978 by Ron Nachman, who served as founding mayor for over 30 years. Demographically it is distinct from most settlements: heavily Russian-speaking olim and a strong French-speaking population, mixed religious-secular (datlash and chiloni majority), populated by workers and academics rather than ideologues. Ariel University, originally a college, gained full university status in 2012. The status remains controversial internationally but is recognized by the Council for Higher Education-Yehuda and Shomron.

Cultural note — Ariel feels like a working-class development town transposed into Samaria. Russian is heard as much as Hebrew at the supermarket; French as much as either in the cafes near the university. The Trans-Samaria highway (Maaleh Israel, kvish 5) connects Ariel directly to Petach Tikva and Tel Aviv, and many residents commute. The university has been BDS-targeted abroad but routinely admits around 17,000 students, including Arab Israelis. Ron Nachman\'s vision was a normal Israeli city, not a religious enclave.
