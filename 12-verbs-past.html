Past-tense Verbs

Lesson 12 · פעלים בעבר

In the past tense, Hebrew verbs take suffixes that change with the person. Each verb has 7 distinct forms (vs. 4 in present). The good news: the root stays recognizable and the endings are consistent.

No pronoun needed — Past-tense endings already show the person. לָמַדְתִּי (lamadti) already means "I learned" — no need for אֲנִי.

Pattern: לָמַד (lamad — to learn)

The Verb "to BE" in Past Tense (היה — haya)

"Was / had been" — In present, "to be" disappears. In past, it returns with the verb הָיָה (haya, was).
