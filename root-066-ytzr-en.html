Root י־צ־ר — creating, forming, drive

Roots Atlas 66 · שֹׁרֶשׁ י צ ר · the creating / forming root
Home י צ ר Morphology
י צ ר
y · tz · r
everything to do with shaping something into being
13 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

This root means to shape matter into form: the potter at his wheel, the factory turning out goods, the artist making a work. The same three letters give the verb "to create", the noun for creation itself, the product on the shelf, the creature that was shaped, and — surprisingly — the יֵצֶר, the inner drive or impulse that shapes us from within. Forming and being formed, the maker and the made, all hang on י-צ-ר. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִיצֹרlitzor
simple active — the plain action
to create, to form
Nif'al
לְהִוָּצֵרlehivatzer
passive / middle — it happens to the subject
to be created, to come into being
Pi'el
לְיַצֵּרleyatzer
intensive / specialized active
to produce, to manufacture

Notice the split of labour: pa'al לִיצֹר is the high register "to create" (the artist, the divine), while pi'el לְיַצֵּר is the industrial "to manufacture". The intensive pi'el often pushes a root toward repeated, large-scale action — here, factory output. The causative hif'il is not lexicalized for this root.
