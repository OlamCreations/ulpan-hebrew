Kashrut — The Laws in Detail

Lesson 115 · כַּשְׁרוּת
Home115

Beyond "no pork, no shellfish" — kashrut is a 3000-year-old food code that touches every meal, every supermarket aisle, every restaurant in Israel. Even the most chiloni Israelis recognize the rules: meat and milk separation, kashering meat (salting), kosher slaughter (shechita), the four signs of kosher animals, the certification system. Reading a kashrut label is a daily literacy skill in Israel.

Cultural note — In Israel, the Chief Rabbinate (הָרַבָּנוּת הָרָאשִׁית) controls the official kashrut certification. There are also private "mehadrin" (more strict) certifications: Eda Charedit, Beit Yosef, OU, etc. Some Israelis avoid Rabbinate-certified products in favor of mehadrin; others eat at any kosher place. McDonald\'s in Israel has both kosher and non-kosher branches.
