Root ב־ש־ל — cooking & ripening

ב ש ל
b · sh · l
everything to do with cooking and with becoming ripe
5 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

The single idea here is becoming ready by heat or time. Fruit on the branch ripens; food on the fire is cooked; a plan that has had time matures. The plain verb is to ripen, the intensive is to cook (you actively boil it), and the causative is to bring to ripeness. From the same letters come the adjective "ripe / ready" and the noun for cooking itself. Every word below is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִבְשֹׁלlivshol
simple active — the plain action
to ripen (intransitive)
Pi'el
לְבַשֵּׁלlevashel
intensive / specialized active
to cook, to boil
Hif'il
לְהַבְשִׁילlehavshil
causative — make someone do it
to ripen, to mature (bring to ripeness)

Three binyanim share the labor cleanly. The pa'al is what happens by itself (fruit ripens); the pi'el is the active, hands-on version (you cook the food); the hif'il is causative (the sun ripens the fruit, an idea is brought to maturity). The passive moulds are not lexicalized here.

Words born from the root
