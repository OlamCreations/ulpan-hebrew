Root נ־ת־ן — giving

נ ת ן
n · t · n
everything to do with giving and what is given
6 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three letters, one open hand: to give. The verb sits at the heart of daily Hebrew (it even doubles as "to let, to allow" — to give permission). From the act of giving come the things given: a gift (מַתָּנָה), and, abstractly, the givens of a problem — נְתוּנִים, the data. When something is given, it becomes נָתוּן: a fact you are handed and must work with. The root is irregular — both נ letters drop in the infinitive לָתֵת — but the meaning stays wide open. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

to give, to allow

לְהִנָּתֵןlehinaten
passive / middle — it happens to the subject

The pa'al לָתֵת is famously irregular: both נ consonants vanish from the infinitive, leaving only תֵת. Its passive partner is the nif'al לְהִנָּתֵן (to be given), which also drifts into "to be possible" — what can be given can be done. The pi'el, hif'il and hitpa'el are not lexicalized for this root.

Words born from the root
