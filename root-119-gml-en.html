Root ג־מ־ל — ripening & requital

ג מ ל
g · m · l
everything to do with ripening, weaning and paying back
7 words · 1 root · 1 verb-voices

One root, two faces that are really one. The verb means to ripen, to wean, to bring a thing to completion, and from there to pay back what completion deserves: reward or punishment, both. Wean a child, ripen a fruit, requite a deed, kick a habit. The famous camel sits in the same family, the animal that goes the distance and comes back. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִגְמֹלligmol
simple active — the plain action
to recompense, to pay back; to wean, to ripen

This root is mostly a noun factory. Only the pa'al verb is lexicalized in everyday Hebrew; the other binyanim are not in common use. The whole web of meaning is carried by the nouns below.

Words born from the root
