Bedouin Society of the Negev

Lesson 184 · בֶּדוּאִים
Home184

About 250,000 Bedouin live in the Negev — Arab Muslim citizens with a distinct tribal heritage. Half live in 7 government-recognized cities and towns (Rahat is the largest), half in dozens of unrecognized villages without official infrastructure. The community is in transition — from semi-nomadic herding to urban-suburban life within two generations. Knowing Bedouin society is essential for any agricultural or KKL work in the Negev — they are your neighbors, the landscape\'s longest-standing inhabitants.

Cultural note — Bedouin tribal structure (hamula = clan, sheikh = leader) remains strong even in cities. Hospitality (deyafa) is a core value — guests are offered coffee, tea, and food without question. Some Bedouin men volunteer in the IDF, notably the Bedouin tracker units. The unrecognized villages debate is central: basic services vs. land claims, an ongoing legal and social tension.
