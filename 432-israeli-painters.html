Modern Israeli painting begins with the 1920s wave (Reuven Rubin, Anna Ticho, Nachum Gutman) painting the Mediterranean light of a young Tel Aviv, moves through the New Horizons (Ofakim Hadashim) modernist break of 1948 (Zaritsky, Streichman, Stematsky), absorbs the German-Jewish exiles (Ardon), and reaches contemporary figures (Yair Garbuz, Tsibi Geva, Michal Heiman). This lesson walks the canon — names, dates, signatures, and where to see the work.

Cultural note — The "Want of Matter" (Dalut Hachomer) was the Israeli answer to American Pop and European Arte Povera in the 1960s-70s — using cardboard, plywood, jute, broken concrete. Raffi Lavie was its champion. When you see a painting in Tel Aviv that looks like a deliberately ugly children\'s collage with Hebrew letters, you\'re looking at a Lavie or his school. It is not "bad painting" — it is a defining national aesthetic.
