Israeli photography sits at the intersection of war reportage and contemporary art. It begins with the 1948 War of Independence — Robert Capa, Werner Braun — moves through David Rubinger\'s 1967 paratroopers at the Kotel and Micha Bar-Am\'s decades for Magnum, and arrives at internationally-shown contemporary artists like Adi Nes, Rina Castelnuovo, and Pavel Wolberg. This lesson covers the canon, key institutions, and the vocabulary you need to read a museum wall text or a Haaretz photo essay.

Cultural note — David Rubinger\'s 1967 photograph of three paratroopers at the Western Wall (Yitzhak Yifat in the centre) is the single most-reproduced Israeli image. Most Israelis have seen it on stamps, in textbooks, on memorial walls. Knowing it by name — "Three Paratroopers at the Kotel" — is basic visual literacy here.
