From a single black-and-white state channel in 1968 to one of the world top format exporters per capita, Israeli television compressed sixty years of evolution into a brief, intense arc. Hatufim seeded Homeland. Fauda put Lior Raz on Netflix. Shtisel made Haredi Brooklyn cry. Tehran won the International Emmy. This lesson gives you the channels, the shows, the creators, and the industry vocabulary to hold a real conversation about Israeli TV with anyone in Tel Aviv or Jerusalem.

Cultural note — Israeli TV punches far above its weight. With a domestic audience of ~9 million, the country produces formats that get adapted in 50+ markets. Why: small market forces low budgets and high concept density. Hatufim was made for ~$200K per episode; Homeland US version, ~$3M. Constraint creates compression, compression creates concept. When Netflix and Apple TV+ buy Israeli originals, they buy that concept density.
