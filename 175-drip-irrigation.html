Drip Irrigation — The Israeli Invention

Lesson 175 · הַשְׁקָאָה בְּטִפְטוּף
Home175

Drip irrigation (טִפְטוּף) was invented in Israel — by Simcha Blass, who in 1959 noticed a tree thriving next to a leaky pipe. He partnered with Kibbutz Hatzerim to found Netafim in 1965 — now a global giant. Drip irrigation uses 50-70% less water than flood irrigation while increasing yields. It\'s the foundation of Israel\'s desert agriculture and one of the country\'s greatest agricultural exports.

Cultural note — When Israelis tell the Netafim story, it\'s with national pride: a small kibbutz in the desert, a clever engineer, a problem solved that changed world agriculture. Knowing the story and vocabulary signals you\'re engaged with the country\'s technical history. Netafim, Naan-Dan-Jain, and Plastro are the major Israeli drip irrigation companies — global market shares ~50%+.
