Yaakov & The Twelve Tribes

Lesson 103 · יַעֲקֹב וְשְׁנֵים עָשָׂר הַשְּׁבָטִים
Home103

Genesis 25-50 minus Yosef. Yaakov tricks his brother Esav for the birthright, dreams of the ladder at Beit El, works 14 years for Lavan to marry Rachel and Leah, wrestles a stranger at the Yabok river and gets renamed Yisrael — "he who struggled with God." His twelve sons become the twelve tribes that name modern Israeli neighborhoods, regions, and synagogues.

Cultural note — Israel's name comes from this story: יִשְׂרָאֵל = "one who wrestled with God." It's literally the foundational identity claim of the country. The 12 tribes' symbols appear on the menorah at the Knesset and on stained-glass windows by Marc Chagall at Hadassah Hospital, Jerusalem.

Key Vocabulary

Modern Place Names from the Story

The Ladder Dream — Genesis 28:10-15

Yaakov flees from Esav and stops for the night at a place that he will name Beit El. Stones for a pillow. The ladder. The angels. The promise.

Wrestling at the Yabok — Genesis 32:25-31

The night before meeting Esav again after 20 years. A stranger wrestles Yaakov until dawn and renames him Yisrael. The site is called Peniel — "face of God."
