Idioms with Animals

Lesson 95 · ביטויים עם בעלי חיים
Home95

Animals carry rich symbolism in Hebrew. From biblical lions and donkeys to modern slang involving cats and chickens, these idioms reveal how Israelis see character, intelligence, behavior.

Cultural reality — Calling someone a חֲמוֹר (donkey) means "stubborn idiot" — strong insult. Calling them a חֲתוּל (cat) can mean "sly clever." Animal idioms are highly emotional. Use carefully.
