To negate in Hebrew: place לֹא (lo) before the verb or adjective. To ask: no inversion needed — just raise your voice at the end (intonation), or start with a question word (who, what, where, when, why, how).

Question Words

Yes/no questions — In spoken Hebrew, keep normal word order and raise your voice. אַתָּה אוֹכֵל? (ata ochel?) = are you eating? The optional word הַאִם (ha'im) marks a formal question.

Negation with "Not Have" — אֵין (eyn)

Have / not have — Hebrew has no verb "to have". You say "there is to me": יֵשׁ לִי (yesh li, I have). And "there is not to me": אֵין לִי (eyn li, I don't have).
