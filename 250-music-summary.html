Block 25 closes the music section. Israel\'s music landscape is a living mosaic: Mizrahi pop reigns popular (Eyal Golan, Sarit Hadad, Omer Adam), rock and alt have their devoted niche (Mashina, HaYehudim, Aviv Geffen), classical is institutional with the IPO and the Mehta legacy, hip-hop has matured into its own scene (Tuna, Dudu Faruk), festivals run year-round (Tamar, Jacob\'s Ladder), Eurovision is a national obsession with 4 wins, and traditional songs anchor cultural memory (Naomi Shemer, Ofra Haza). Across nine lessons we covered all these areas. This summary consolidates the 10 essential terms, key institutions, key artists, and the ideological pairs that map the field.

Cultural note — Music is the universal Israeli social currency. Knowing this vocabulary lets you navigate weddings (Mizrahi), dinner parties (rock and folk), formal evenings (classical), bars (hip-hop), festivals (eclectic). The cultural canon you should know: Yerushalayim shel Zahav (Naomi Shemer), Im Nin\'alu (Ofra Haza), Hallelujah (Eurovision 1979), Toy (Netta 2018), Anachnu Lo Tzrichim (Mashina), Tel Aviv (Omer Adam). Master these six songs and you\'ll find yourself singing along at every gathering.
