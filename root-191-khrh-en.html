Root ח־ר־ה: burning anger

Roots Atlas 191 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ח ר ה · the burning-anger root
Home ח ר ה Morphology
ח ר ה
ch · r · h
everything that flares up: wrath, and the rivalry that catches fire
7 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

At its core ח־ר־ה means to burn, to flare up. The Bible says חרה אף, literally "the nose burned", for someone whose anger ignites. From that heat the root branches in two directions: the old wrath sense (חָרוֹן, burning indignation), and a modern twist where the same fire becomes competition. To compete (לְהִתְחָרוֹת) is to heat up against a rival. One image, anger as fire, feeds both the courtroom and the racetrack. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַחֲרוֹתlacharot
simple active, the plain action
to be angry, to burn with anger (biblical, חרה אף)
Hif'il
לְהַחְרוֹתlehachrot
causative, make someone do it
to concur, to side with (rare)
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְחָרוֹתlehitcharot
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to compete (against), to vie

The pa'al here is biblical and rarely heard in speech; modern Hebrew keeps the heat alive almost entirely through the hitpa'el לְהִתְחָרוֹת (to compete). The hif'il sense "to concur" is a separate, marginal usage. No pi'el, pu'al, nif'al or huf'al are lexicalized for this root.
