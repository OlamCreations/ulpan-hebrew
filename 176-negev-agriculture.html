Agriculture in the Negev — Desert Made Productive

Lesson 176 · חַקְלָאוּת בְּנֶגֶב
Home176

The Negev covers 60% of Israel's land but only 9% of its population. Yet it produces 60% of the country\'s vegetables and is a global R&D hub for desert agriculture. Ramat HaNegev R&D station, Yair Station, and dozens of kibbutzim make crops grow in 200mm/year rainfall. KKL\'s post-alyah formation often funnels people here. This is your direct professional landscape post-aliyah.

Cultural note — The Negev was Ben-Gurion's obsession ("the Negev is where the Jewish people will be tested") and remains the strategic land of Israeli development. Hemp pilots, solar farms, dairy, vineyards, exotic crops (mango, dragon fruit, pomegranate) — all are grown there. KKL\'s Sde Boker training program, Ramat HaNegev R&D, and the Arava International Center for Agricultural Training are recognized worldwide.
