Root א־ה־ל: tent

This is a small, ancient root, and in everyday Hebrew it has come down to almost a single word: the tent. It is one of the oldest dwelling-words in the language, the portable home of the desert. Rather than spreading into a busy family of verbs, it stayed concrete: the tent itself. The page is short on purpose, because the verified family is short. Every word here is checked against pealim, niqqud included.

No everyday verb is lexicalized for this root: there is a biblical "to pitch a tent", but modern Hebrew leaves א-ה-ל as a noun and says "to put up a tent" with another verb. So this page is noun-only, which is perfectly normal. A root is not obliged to produce verbs.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
