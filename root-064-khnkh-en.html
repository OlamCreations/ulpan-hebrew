Root ח־נ־ך — educating / inaugurating

Roots Atlas 64 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ח נ ך · the educating root
Home ח נ ך Morphology
ח נ ך
ch · n · kh
everything to do with starting something on its proper path
8 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

One idea runs underneath: starting something on its right path for the first time. Start a child on the way and you educate them; start a building into use and you inaugurate it. These are the same act in Hebrew, which is why חִנּוּךְ (education) and חֲנֻכָּה (the festival of re-dedicating the Temple) are siblings. Even the eight-branch chanukiyah is named for that first lighting. Each word below is a first-beginning of one kind or another, verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַחֲנֹךְlachanoch
simple active — the plain action
to inaugurate, to dedicate
Nif'al
לְהֵחָנֵךְlehechanech
passive / middle — it happens to the subject
to be inaugurated
Pi'el
לְחַנֵּךְlechanech
intensive / specialized active
to educate, to teach, to bring up

The two senses divide by binyan: pa'al לַחֲנֹךְ inaugurates a thing (a building, a season), while pi'el לְחַנֵּךְ inaugurates a person into life — that is what education is. The nif'al is the plain passive of the inauguration. No pu'al/huf'al/hitpa'el are lexicalized here, which is normal.
