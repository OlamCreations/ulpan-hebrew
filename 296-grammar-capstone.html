By B2/C1 you control the seven binyanim, smichut chains, conditional clauses, modal constructions, passive voice, and noun-adjective gender/number agreement automatically. This capstone consolidates the trickiest constructions: future-tense polite forms, contrary-to-fact conditionals (lu / ilmaleh), high-register passives, and the "yesh + infinitive" obligation pattern. If you can produce these without thinking, you\'re solidly B2.

Cultural note — Native Israelis often skip strict grammar in casual speech (e.g. confusing accusative "et" with direct object). At B2/C1 you\'re expected to know the rules even when natives don\'t. Formal writing requires precision: smichut accuracy, gender agreement, correct binyan choice. Spoken Hebrew tolerates much more flexibility — "שלוש שקלים" (m/f mismatch) is common in shuk talk.
