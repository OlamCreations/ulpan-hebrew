Root ד־ב־ק: sticking & devotion

Roots Atlas 120 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ד ב ק · the sticking / devotion root

ד ב ק
d · v · k
everything to do with sticking, clinging and cleaving
8 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

Three letters for one gesture: sticking together. Physically it is glue and adhesion; biologically it is contagion, one body catching what another carries; spiritually it is dvekut, the soul cleaving to God. The same verb that glues a stamp describes a follower clinging to a teacher. Even the haunting dybbuk is a spirit that will not let go. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִדְבֹּקlidbok
simple active, the plain action
to stick, to adhere, to cling
Nif'al
לְהִדָּבֵקlehidavek
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to stick (to); to get infected, to catch (a disease)
Hif'il
לְהַדְבִּיקlehadbik
causative, make someone do it
to glue, to stick (cause to adhere); to infect

Watch the voice shift: pa'al sticks of itself, nif'al gets stuck or gets infected (passive / spontaneous), hif'il makes something stick or passes the infection on (causative). One root, the whole grammar of contagion.
