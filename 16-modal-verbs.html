Modal & Useful Verbs

Lesson 16 · פעלי עזר ושימושיים

Verbs like "can, must, want, like, need" are essential for expressing intent, obligation, taste. In Hebrew, they're often combined with an infinitive (לְ + verb). Plus the most common everyday verbs.

Infinitive — Hebrew infinitive starts with לְ (le-, "to / for"). לִשְׁתּוֹת (lishtot) = to drink. אֲנִי רוֹצֶה לִשְׁתּוֹת = I want to drink.

Can / Be Permitted (יָכוֹל / אֶפְשָׁר)

Must / Need (חַיָּב / צָרִיךְ / מֻכְרָח)

Nuances — צָרִיךְ (tzarich) = need / have to (most used) · חַיָּב (chayav) = obligated (stronger) · מֻכְרָח (muchrach) = compelled (formal).

Like / Love (אוֹהֵב)

Want (רוֹצֶה)

Do / Make (עוֹשֶׂה)

Give / Receive (נוֹתֵן / מְקַבֵּל)

Understand (מֵבִין)

Think (חוֹשֵׁב)

Sleep / Wake / Rise
