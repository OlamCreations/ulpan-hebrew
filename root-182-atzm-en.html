Root ע־צ־ם — substance & strength

ע צ ם
' · tz · m
everything to do with the bone, the self, and sheer power
10 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root, one hard core. The bone is the body's solid frame, and from "the solid thing in itself" Hebrew draws two more meanings: the essence of a thing, even the word for -self, and the family of strength, power and intensity. To be עַצְמָאִי is to stand on your own bones, independent. A nation's independence and a great power both grow from this single root. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַעֲצֹםla'atzom
simple active — the plain action
to shut, to close (one's eyes)
Hif'il
לְהַעֲצִיםleha'atzim
causative — make someone do it
to strengthen, to intensify, to amplify

The pa'al keeps an old concrete sense ("to shut the eyes", to make them firm); the hif'il is the living modern verb, causative "to make stronger, to empower". The nif'al, pi'el, pu'al, huf'al and hitpa'el verb slots are not lexicalized, though the hitpa'el surfaces as a noun below.
