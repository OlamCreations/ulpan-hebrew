Reading a Hebrew Cookbook

Lesson 147 · קְרִיאַת סֵפֶר בִּשּׁוּל
Home147

Decoding a Hebrew recipe is its own skill. Imperative verbs (chop! stir! bake!), Hebrew measurements (כַּף, כַּפִּית, כּוֹס), Israeli units (גְּרָם, מ"ל), oven temperatures in Celsius. Lesson 40 introduced cooking; this one teaches you to read any Israeli cookbook — Erez Komarovsky, Eyal Shani, Yotam Ottolenghi (who writes mostly in English but with deep Hebrew kitchen roots).

Cultural note — The Israeli cookbook canon: Janna Gur\'s "The Book of New Israeli Food" (English + Hebrew), Erez Komarovsky\'s bread books, Ezra Kedem\'s Levantine fusion. Online: "מַתְכּוֹנֵי שֶׁל" (Cooking with Sef), "אֲנִי שֶׁף" (I\'m Chef) — typical Hebrew recipe sites with active comment culture.
