Root ח־פ־שׂ: searching, freedom & vacation

ח פ ש
ch · f · s
everything to do with searching, freedom, vacation, disguise
7 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Here one root holds two ideas that secretly rhyme: to search and to be free. You search for something; freedom is the search set loose. The same letters give the verb "to search", the noun "freedom", the holidays when you are off, and the adjective "free". The hitpa'el even adds dressing-up: to disguise yourself is to search out another identity, hence the Purim costume. One root runs from the lab search to the carnival mask. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el

Hitpa'el
לְהִתְחַפֵּשׂlehitchapes

to disguise oneself (as someone)

This root lives in the intensive binyanim, not the plain pa'al: searching is a pi'el (an active, deliberate hunt), and disguising oneself is its hitpa'el reflexive (searching out a new self). The other binyanim are not lexicalized here.
