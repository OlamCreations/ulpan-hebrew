Cinema in Hebrew — the language of production, of frames and fades, of genres and screens. Israel has a vibrant film industry: the Jerusalem Cinematheque, the Haifa International Film Festival, the Ophir Awards (the Israeli Oscars). This lesson gives you the vocabulary to read a review, discuss a film with friends, work on a set, or argue about which director deserved the prize. From במאי to פסקול, from תקריב to קדימון.

Cultural note — Israeli cinema punches above its weight. Films like Waltz with Bashir, Foxtrot, and Synonyms have won at Cannes, Berlin, and Venice. The Cinematheques in Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa, and Holon are cultural institutions — not just movie theaters but archives, schools, and meeting points. Knowing the vocabulary opens doors to a real community of cinephiles, critics, and filmmakers.
