Root ש־כ־ר: wage & hire

Roots Atlas 100 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ש כ ר · the wage / renting root
Home ש כ ר Morphology
ש כ ר
s / sh · k · r
everything to do with renting, wages and hiring
12 words · 1 root · 4 verb-voices

These three letters spell two roots that look alike but split on the first letter. With שׂ (sin) the idea is paying for use: to rent, to hire, to earn a wage, שָׂכָר (pay), מַשְׂכֹּרֶת (salary), שָׂכִיר (a salaried worker), שָׂכוּר (rented). With שׁ (shin) the very same consonants turn to intoxication: לְשַׁכֵּר (to make drunk), שִׁכּוֹר (a drunkard). Modern Hebrew treats them as one written family with two threads. The wage thread is built from the pa'al / hif'il pair (you hire, you let out); the drink thread from the pi'el / hitpa'el pair (you intoxicate, you get drunk). Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִשְׂכֹּרliskor
simple active, the plain action
to rent, to hire (take on hire)
Pi'el
לְשַׁכֵּרleshaker
intensive / specialized active
to inebriate, to intoxicate
Hif'il
לְהַשְׂכִּירlehaskir
causative, make someone do it
to rent out, to let (a property)
Hitpa'el
לְהִשְׁתַּכֵּרlehishtaker
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to get drunk

Two clean contrasts live here. On the wage thread (שׂ), pa'al לִשְׂכֹּר is renting in (you take a flat) while hif'il לְהַשְׂכִּיר is the causative "renting out" (you make it available to a tenant). On the drink thread (שׁ), pi'el לְשַׁכֵּר is making someone else drunk, and hitpa'el לְהִשְׁתַּכֵּר is the reflexive getting-yourself-drunk. Same skeleton, opposite directions.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
