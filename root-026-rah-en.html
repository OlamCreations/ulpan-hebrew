Root ר־א־ה — seeing

ר א ה
r · ' · h
everything to do with seeing
11 words · 1 root · 4 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: seeing. The plain verb sees; the passive is visible or seems; the causative shows; the reciprocal is two people seeing each other again — the everyday goodbye lehitra'ot. The nouns then name what seeing produces: the eyesight itself, the mirror that throws sight back, the proof that makes a claim visible to reason, even the interview where two people size each other up. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Nif'al
לְהֵרָאוֹתlehera'ot
passive / middle — it happens to the subject

Hif'il
לְהַרְאוֹתlehar'ot
causative — make someone do it
to show, to demonstrate
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְרָאוֹתlehitra'ot
reflexive / reciprocal — to / on oneself
to see one another again, to meet ("see you!")

Four binyanim are live and map cleanly onto voice: pa'al sees, nif'al is seen/seems, hif'il causes seeing (shows), hitpa'el is reciprocal (see each other). The pi'el, pu'al and huf'al slots are not lexicalized for this root.
