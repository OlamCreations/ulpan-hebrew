Signing Off — Endings & Farewells

Lesson 299 · סִיוּמִים וּפְרִידוֹת
Home299

Hebrew has a wide range of closing phrases — for emails, calls, in-person goodbyes, formal documents, and ritual occasions. Knowing the right closer signals register awareness as much as the right opener. From "lehitra\'ot" (until we see again, casual) to "be\'kavod rav" (with great respect, formal letter) to "shavu\'a tov" (good week, after Shabbat). This lesson catalogs the full toolkit, including the religious closures and the slang shortcuts.

Cultural note — Israelis don\'t end conversations as abruptly as they start them. A typical farewell sequence: "yallah" (let\'s wrap up) → "tihiyeh li bari" (be well) → "lehitra\'ot" (see you) → "bye" (English borrowed). Religious people add "ad biat ha-mashiach" (until the messiah\'s arrival) on holidays. After Shabbat, "shavu\'a tov" replaces all other farewells until Sunday morning. WhatsApp culture has shrunk farewells to single emojis or "👋", but B2/C1 means knowing the full forms.
