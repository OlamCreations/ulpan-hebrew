Conversation Capstone — Real-World Fluency

Lesson 298 · סִכּוּם שִׂיחָה
Home298

B2/C1 conversation means navigating any social context: small talk with a stranger, intense political debate, technical discussion at work, family argument, taxi-driver chat about football. You can interrupt politely, change topic, hold opinions, disagree without offense, recover from mishearings. This lesson catalogs the conversational moves that mark advanced fluency — interjections, hedging, backchanneling, register-shifting on the fly.

Cultural note — Israeli conversation is notoriously direct: interruption is normal, opinions stated forcefully, "lo nakhon" (not correct) said without apology. B2/C1 means joining in — not just following along. Practice voicing disagreement: "אני לא בטוח", "זה לא בדיוק ככה", "אבל מה לגבי...". Israelis respect speakers who push back. Quiet listeners get talked over.
