Root ק־נ־ה: buying & acquiring

Roots Atlas 200 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ק נ ה · the acquisition root
Home ק נ ה Morphology
ק נ ה
k · n · h
everything to do with buying, acquiring, and making something your own
6 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

At the core is one act: to acquire, to make a thing pass into your hands. The everyday face is buying (לִקְנוֹת), but the root reaches wider than money, you can also acquire a skill, a habit, a reputation. The causative לְהַקְנוֹת flips it to giving someone an acquisition: to impart, to instill (a teacher imbues a student with knowledge). And the shopping mall, קַנְיוֹן, is a modern coinage built straight on "buying". One root, from a loaf of bread to a whole education.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִקְנוֹתliknot
simple active, the plain action
to buy, to purchase, to acquire
Nif'al
לְהִקָּנוֹתlehikanot
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be bought, to be acquired
Hif'il
לְהַקְנוֹתlehaknot
causative, make someone do it
to impart, to instill, to give (someone) an acquisition

Three live verbs map cleanly onto voice: pa'al = I acquire, nif'al = it gets acquired (passive), hif'il = I cause someone to acquire (to impart). The pi'el and the passives of the causative are not lexicalized here.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
