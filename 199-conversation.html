The penultimate lesson focuses on real conversational Hebrew across all situations: shop interactions, doctor visits, banking, dating, professional meetings, casual chat. After 198 lessons of vocabulary, grammar, and cultural context, this lesson assembles it all into the actual rhythm of Israeli speech. Conversational Hebrew is fast, idiom-saturated, and assumes shared cultural context. This is the layer that proves you can live in Israel. Master this and the country opens up.

Cultural note — Israelis are direct. They interrupt, they say "no" without softening, they assume you can take banter. They greet with shalom or ahalan, drop quickly into first names, and end with shalom or ciao. The cultural value of "kushiya" (asking the right question) is high.
