Lesson 459 · אוֹצַר מִלִּים שֶׁל מָחוֹל
Home459

This is the deep vocabulary lesson for navigating Israeli dance professionally — class language, reviews, programme texts, audition feedback, body-anatomy specifics, and the working language of the rehearsal studio. Hebrew dance vocabulary borrows from French (ballet terms), English (modern), Russian (technique), and Arabic-Yemenite (folk-dance gestures). This lesson sorts the field for readers — students, parents, journalists, audience members.

Cultural note — Real working studios run in three languages simultaneously: Hebrew commands ("achshav!" — "now!"), French ballet terms ("plié, tendu, dégagé"), and English Gaga cues ("thick spaghetti, soft float"). A foreign visitor with strong English and a few Hebrew words can follow class. Knowing the rehearsal-Hebrew register lets you read post-show reviews in Haaretz and the artist statements on Suzanne Dellal\'s website without struggle.
