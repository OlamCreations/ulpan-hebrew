Root פ־ר־ה: being fruitful

פ ר ה
p · r · h
bearing fruit and multiplying
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One agricultural image runs through this small but ancient family: bearing fruit. The verb liprot ("be fruitful and multiply") opens the Bible; the noun pri is simply fruit; and what is poreh is fertile, productive. Push the verb into the causative and you get to fertilize, and from there modern Hebrew names lab fertilization, hafraya. Watch the same seed-idea move from Genesis to a fertility clinic. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִפְרוֹתliprot
simple active, the plain action
to be fruitful, to multiply (biblical)
Hif'il
לְהַפְרוֹתlehafrot
causative, make someone do it
to fertilize (cause to bear fruit)

The pair is built on cause: pa'al = (something) bears fruit by itself; hif'il = (someone) makes it bear fruit. This is a final-ה (lamed-he) root, so the infinitives end in ־וֹת rather than the usual pattern. No passive partner is lexicalized; the result-side lives in the nouns below.
