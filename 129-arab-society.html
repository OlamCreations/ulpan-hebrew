Arab Israeli Society

Lesson 129 · הָחֶבְרָה הָעֲרָבִית בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל
Home129

~21% of Israeli citizens are Arab — Muslim, Christian, Druze, Bedouin. They speak Arabic at home, Hebrew at work, often read both. They serve (Druze, some Bedouin) or don\'t serve (most) in the IDF. They voted Mansour Abbas into a coalition government for the first time in 2021. Knowing the basic landscape — towns, communities, parties, key issues — is essential for understanding modern Israel.

Cultural note — The naming question is itself political: "Israeli Arabs" / "Palestinian citizens of Israel" / "Arabs of \'48" — each phrase signals a political position. The 1976 "Land Day" (Yom Ha-Adama) is commemorated annually after 6 Arab citizens were killed protesting land expropriation. Most Arab citizens use both Hebrew and Arabic fluently; in mixed cities like Haifa, Jaffa, Ramla, Akko, Lod — daily life mixes both languages.

Yom Ha-Adama (Land Day) — March 30, 1976

Annual commemoration of 6 Arab citizens killed during protests against land expropriation in the Galilee, March 30, 1976. The defining event of Arab Israeli political consciousness.

Mixed-City Hebrew (Haifa / Jaffa / Akko)

In mixed cities, Hebrew is interspersed with Arabic loanwords daily. This is what coexistence sounds like at a falafel stand in Wadi Nisnas, Haifa.
