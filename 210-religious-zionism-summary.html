Block 21 closes with a synthesis. Religious Zionism (~12-20% of Israeli Jews) holds outsized influence in IDF, settlements, and ministerial cabinets. The 10 lessons traced: yesha geography, Gush Etzion history, hesder programs, Mercaz HaRav and its lineage, Rav Kook\'s mystical Zionism, Ariel as a hybrid city, kippa srugah identity, Bennett as modernist face, Ne\'emanei Eretz Yisrael as ideological hard core. This summary consolidates the 10 most-used terms, key institutions, key figures, ideological pairs.

Cultural note — You\'ll meet religious Zionists everywhere in Israel: in Yesha but also Beit Shemesh, Modi\'in, Gush Dan suburbs (Givat Shmuel), academic circles, IDF officer corps. Their political vehicle has fragmented (Bayit HaYehudi → Yamina → various splinters), but the cultural bloc persists. Knowing the lexicon means you can navigate Yom Yerushalayim ceremonies, Bnei Akiva chapters, hesder discussions, settlement-vs-state debates without confusion. After Block 21 you can read religious-Zionist op-eds in Makor Rishon and follow the discourse.
