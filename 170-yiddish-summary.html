Closing Block 17. Yiddish is not a separate language for most Israelis — it\'s the emotional substrate beneath Hebrew. Knowing it lets you decode jokes, understand grandmothers, follow ultra-Orthodox news, and hear the irony in cabinet meetings. From Sholem Aleichem to Eretz Nehederet to a klezmer bar in Florentin — Yiddish is the warmth running through Israeli life.

Cultural note — A Yiddish-aware Israeli knows when someone is being warm vs. ironic, when they\'re catastrophizing for fun vs. seriously, and when they\'re using English-Hebrew-Yiddish to sound cosmopolitan vs. ironic. This is not just vocabulary — it\'s social intelligence. Olim who absorb the Yiddish layer feel "more Israeli" than vocabulary alone could deliver.
