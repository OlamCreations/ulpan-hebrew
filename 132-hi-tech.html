Hi-Tech & The Startup Nation

Lesson 132 · הִיטֵק וְ-Startup Nation
Home132

~10% of Israeli workforce, ~50% of exports, ~25% of corporate tax revenue. Concentrated in Tel Aviv, Herzliya, Ramat Gan, Beersheba. The pipeline runs through 8200 (military intel), Talpiot (elite officer track), the Technion, Hebrew U, Tel Aviv U. Major exits: Mobileye ($15.3B to Intel, 2017), Waze ($1.1B to Google, 2013), Wix ($600M IPO, 2013). Acquisition-led, not Apple-style platform-led.

Cultural note — "אֶקְזִיט" (exit) is a normal Hebrew word now, with no equivalent native term. So is "סְטָארְט-אַפּ" (start-up), "פִּיצָ\'\'ר" (pitch), "רַאוּנְד" (round). The Israeli founder cliché: ex-8200, mid-30s, second startup, lives between Tel Aviv and Palo Alto. The phrase "אֵיזֶה רַאוּנְד גִּיוּסְתֶּם?" — "what round did you raise?" — is normal small talk on Rothschild.
