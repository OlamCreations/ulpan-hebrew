Lesson 160 · סִכּוּם צה"ל

Closing Block 16 with synthesis. The IDF is more than a military — it\'s the central socializing institution of Israeli society. It produces the country\'s elite (8200 → tech), its political leaders, its cultural references. The phrase "אַחֲרֵי הַצָּבָא" — "after the army" — marks adulthood. Knowing IDF Hebrew lets you decode the country\'s core social grammar.

Cultural note — Israelis under 50 mostly served. The army experience is the universal Jewish-Israeli reference frame — even haredim and Arabs who don\'t serve refer to "the army" as the central institution shaping society. Reading the room in Israel often means reading military background. The 4 questions: "Where did you serve?" "What rank?" "What unit?" "Officer track?" — answer them, you\'re halfway there.
