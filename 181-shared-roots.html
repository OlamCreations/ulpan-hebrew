Shared Semitic Roots

Lesson 181 · עִבְרִית וְעַרְבִית
Home181

Hebrew and Arabic are both Semitic languages built on the same triliteral root system. Roughly 30-50% of basic vocabulary is cognate: k-t-b means "write" in both, sh-l-m carries "peace/wholeness" in both, b-y-t is "house" in both. Knowing this layer makes Arabic naturally accessible from Hebrew and reveals the deep structure of Hebrew itself. Useful for everyday life in Israel where Arabic is the second official language and ~20% of citizens are Arab. The door to Levantine Arabic opens through the Hebrew you already have.

Cultural note — Native Hebrew speakers often pick up Arabic faster than other languages, and vice versa. The shoresh (root) system is identical: 3 consonants generate verb-noun-adjective families across both tongues. Many Mizrahi Jews who came from Arab countries spoke both natively, blending the two registers in family speech, and modern Israeli slang is half-Yiddish, half-Arabic.
