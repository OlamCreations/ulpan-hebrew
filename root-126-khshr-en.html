Root כ־ש־ר: fitness

Roots Atlas 126 · שֹׁרֶשׁ כ ש ר · the fit / kosher root

כ ש ר
k · sh · r
everything that is fit, qualified, kosher
9 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One idea runs through this whole family: being fit for purpose. The English borrowing "kosher" is just the most famous corner of it. Underneath, the root means qualified, capable, up to standard, whether the standard is religious law (kashrut), professional skill (qualifications), or a body that is in shape (fitness). The verbs let you make something fit, the nouns name the fitness, the certificate, and even the tool that does a job. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִכְשֹׁרlikhshor
simple active, the plain action
to be fit, to be appropriate, to succeed
Hif'il
לְהַכְשִׁירlehakhshir
causative, make someone do it
to qualify, to train; to certify as kosher

The pa'al "to be fit" is rare in everyday speech; the working verb is the hif'il לְהַכְשִׁיר, the causative "to make fit", to train people, to qualify a tool, to make food kosher. Same act, three contexts.
