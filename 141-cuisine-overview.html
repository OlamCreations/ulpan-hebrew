Israeli cuisine is not one tradition but ten. The dishes you order in any Tel Aviv restaurant come from Yemen, Morocco, Iraq, Poland, Romania, Russia, Greece, Tunisia, Persia, and Galilee Arab kitchens. The Israeli "national" dishes — hummus, falafel, shakshuka, sabich — are all Levantine or Mizrahi imports adopted as shared identity. Lesson 14 covered restaurant basics; this block goes deep.

Cultural note — Hummus is not Israeli. It\'s shared Levantine — claimed by Lebanon, Palestine, Syria, Israel. The political fights are real ("hummus wars"). Inside Israel, who makes the best hummus is its own contested topic: "אַבּוּ חַסַן" in Yafo, "אַבּוּ מַארְק" in Haifa, "פִינַת מָאזֶל" in Jerusalem each have die-hard defenders.
