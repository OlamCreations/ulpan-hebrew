Olim & The IDF

Lesson 158 · עוֹלִים בָצְבָא
Home158

If you make aliyah at 18-22, you serve full mandatory term. At 23-26: shorter (~14 months). At 27+: usually exempt or volunteer. Special tracks for olim: Mahal (volunteer foreign nationals), Garin Tzabar (group aliyah for IDF), Lone Soldier (חַיָּיל בּוֹדֵד) status — extra benefits and family-replacement system. Lesson 71 introduced this; here we go deep.

Cultural note — A "lone soldier" (חַיָּיל בּוֹדֵד) is one without family in Israel — usually an oleh, but also some Israelis whose parents are abroad. They get free housing, increased pay, monthly food allowance, weekend "adoptive families" (מִשְׁפָּחַת אִמּוּץ), and a Yom HaAtzma\'ut barbecue at the President\'s house. Nefesh B\'Nefesh and the Lone Soldier Center coordinate. ~7,000 lone soldiers serve at any time.
