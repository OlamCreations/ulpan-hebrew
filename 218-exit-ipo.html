Exit & IPO — The Vocabulary of Liquidity

Lesson 218 · אֵקְזִיט וְהַנְפָּקָה
Home218

"Exit" is the verb of Israeli tech — sell your company or take it public to convert ownership into cash. Two main paths: M&A (acquisition by a strategic or financial buyer) or IPO (listing on a stock exchange). Israeli tech averaged 80-130 exits per year (2020-2023), totaling $20-80B annually. This lesson teaches the vocabulary of cap tables, term sheets, and liquidity events used daily by founders, employees, and VCs.

Cultural note — "Exit" carries a specific cultural meaning in Israel — generational mobility. A successful exit typically transforms 50-200 employees from middle-income to wealthy in one liquidity event. Many Israeli founders cycle through 2-3 exits before "settling" — this is normal, not unusual. Vocabulary like "vesting cliff," "liquidation preference," "drag-along" is part of normal Tel Aviv conversation. If someone says "I had a small exit," they probably mean $5-20M company sale, not their own equity.
