Verbal Nouns by Binyan

Morphology 06 · שֵׁם פְּעֻלָּה

Every binyan has a matching noun that names its action, the שֵׁם פְּעֻלָּה (shem pe'ula, "action-noun"). "He wrote" → "writing". "Succeeded" → "success". "Participated" → "participation". The key fact: the noun's mould tells you which binyan and which verb it came from. So this is a two-way street, verb gives noun, and noun gives back the verb. This is the single most useful table in the track.

Pu'al and Huf'al borrow, the passive binyanim do not coin their own action-nouns in everyday Hebrew. They lean on their active twin's noun (pi'el's _ִי_וּ_, hif'il's הַ_ָ_ָה). So in practice you have five moulds to know, not seven.
Read it backwards, this is the payoff. See a noun ending in -וּת with הִתְ on the front → hitpa'el → there is a reflexive verb behind it. See הַ_ָ_ָה → hif'il → a causative verb. See _ִי_וּ_ → pi'el. The noun is a fingerprint.
