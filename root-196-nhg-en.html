Root נ־ה־ג: driving, leading, custom

Roots Atlas 196 · שֹׁרֶשׁ נ ה ג · the driving / leading root
Home נ ה ג Morphology
נ ה ג
n · h · g
everything about steering a course: driving a vehicle, leading people, the habits and customs we follow
7 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

נ־ה־ג carries one rich idea: to guide a course. The very same pa'al verb (לִנְהֹג) means both to drive a vehicle and to be in the habit of doing something, because both are about the way you steer, whether a car or your own conduct. Push the root into the causative hif'il and you get to lead, to rule. From there it radiates: the driver, the leader, the act of driving, the custom a community follows, and one's overall behaviour. One root quietly governs the road, the nation, and personal habit. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִנְהֹגlinhog
simple active, the plain action
to drive (a vehicle); to be accustomed to, to usually do
Hif'il
לְהַנְהִיגlehanhig
causative, make someone do it
to lead, to rule; to introduce, to institute

The pa'al לִנְהֹג already holds both halves of the root: the literal (to drive) and the figurative (to have a habit / customary way). The hif'il לְהַנְהִיג is causative: to make a course be followed, hence to lead, rule, or institute a practice. The reflexive sense (how one conducts oneself) is carried by the hitpa'el noun הִתְנַהֲגוּת below. No nif'al, pi'el, pu'al or huf'al are lexicalized for this root.
