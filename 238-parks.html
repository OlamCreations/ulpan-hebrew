Parks — Sacher, HaYarkon, and the Outdoor Network

Lesson 238 · פַּרְקִים וּגַנִּים
Home238

Israel's parks combine recreational green spaces with archaeological and history sites. Major urban parks: Park HaYarkon (Tel Aviv, 350 hectares — largest urban park), Park Sacher (Jerusalem, near the Knesset), Park Hadera (north of TLV), Park HaSharon (Petach Tikva). National parks: Caesarea, Masada, Ein Gedi, Banias, Megiddo. Park HaYarkon hosts free concerts (Tahrir stage, large festivals), rents bikes, has a pool. SPNI manages many sites; Israel Nature and Parks Authority manages 70+ national parks.

Cultural note — Parks are central to Israeli leisure: Saturday picnic ("piknik shabbat") with families, kibbutzim renting bikes by the dozen, kids playing soccer everywhere. Park HaYarkon\'s "Tahrir Square" stage hosts everything from Eyal Golan concerts to political rallies. National parks charge entry (~30-50 NIS adults); annual passes via Bituach Briut subsidies for olim. Best parks for kids: Park HaYarkon, Park Hadera. For history: Caesarea, Masada, Megiddo. The lawn culture is real — Israelis sit, talk, snack, never quite leave.
