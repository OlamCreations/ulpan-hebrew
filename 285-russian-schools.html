Russian-Language Schools — NETA, Mofet, Heritage

Lesson 285 · בָּתֵּי סֵפֶר רוּסִיִּים בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל
Home285

Russian-language education in Israel runs across state programs (Mofet, NETA), private after-school programs, and weekend heritage classes. Mofet is the most famous network — built early 1990s for Soviet-trained teachers, ~30 schools, integrating Russian academic standards into Israeli curriculum. Many Russian-Israeli families also send kids to Russian-Israeli kindergartens (gan rusi) and Saturday schools to maintain bilingualism. About half of Russian-Israeli children reach Russian literacy by age 12.

Cultural note — The Mofet network was created to use Soviet-trained teachers and Soviet academic discipline. Mofet schools are highly competitive — heavy math, sciences, chess — and produce many academic stars. Family pressure to maintain Russian and Soviet-style discipline is strong. As the 1.5 generation grew up, support for Russian-language education declined; the second generation increasingly went Hebrew-only. A small revival has appeared post-October 7 with Russian-speaking lone soldiers' families.
