Conditional Sentences

Lesson 27 · משפטי תנאי
Home27

Hebrew has 3 conditional types — real (im), unreal (lu), and counterfactual (ilu). The word for "if" depends on whether the situation is possible (im), hypothetical (lu), or impossible/past (ilu).

3 types of "if" — אִם (im) = if (real, possible) · לוּ (lu) = if (hypothetical, present/future) · אִלּוּ (ilu) = if (counterfactual, past — "had I known...").
