Vowels (Niqqud)

Lesson 02 · ניקוד
Home 01 02 03 04 05

Written Hebrew has only consonants. Vowels are marked by dots and dashes around or below letters — this system is called niqqud. In daily life, Israelis read without niqqud, but it's essential for learning correct pronunciation as a beginner.

Other Vowels

Position — Most niqqudot sit under the letter. Cholam (dot above-left) and Shuruk (vav with dot inside) are exceptions.
Demo on Bet (ב) — The same letter Bet takes different sounds depending on niqqud: בָּ (ba), בֶּ (beh), בִּ (bi), בּוֹ (bo), בּוּ (bu).
