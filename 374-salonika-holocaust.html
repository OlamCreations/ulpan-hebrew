Salonika & Greek Sephardi Holocaust

Lesson 374 · סָלוֹנִיקִי וְהַשּׁוֹאָה הַסְּפָרַדִּית הַיְּוָונִית
Home374

Salonika — the Jerusalem of the Balkans. For 450 years after 1492, an 80,000-strong Sephardi community made Thessaloniki the only major European city where Jews were the plurality, where the port closed on Shabbat, where Ladino was the lingua franca of commerce. In 17 months between March 1943 and August 1944, the Nazis erased it. Of ~50,000 deported to Auschwitz, ~96% were murdered. ~1,300 returned. The community that produced rabbis, merchants, dockworkers, poets, and the Sabbatean false-messianic crisis of 1666 — gone. This lesson covers the rise, the long Greek-era squeeze, and the destruction.

Cultural note — Greek Jews are not a footnote. Salonika was the largest Sephardi community in the world after 1492, larger than any in Spain before the expulsion. The 96% death rate is one of the highest of any European Jewish community, comparable to Polish shtetls. When Israeli Sephardim of Greek origin say "before the war" they mean a vanished civilization, not just a vanished generation. Olim from Greece (~3-4K post-war) brought Ladino, specific liturgical melodies (Maftirim), and a Mediterranean-Levantine cuisine that is now part of Israeli food heritage.
