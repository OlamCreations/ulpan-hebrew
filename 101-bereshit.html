Bereshit Aleph — Creation

Lesson 101 · בְּרֵאשִׁית א
Home101

The opening chapter of the Torah. Seven days, in the most quoted Hebrew passage on Earth. Biblical Hebrew differs from modern in two main ways: vav-consecutive (ויאמר instead of אמר) and word order (verb-subject-object). Niqqud stays on for biblical texts always — the Masoretic vowels are part of the canonical text. Words still alive in modern Hebrew are tagged [modern].

Cultural note — Even chiloni Israelis can recite the first verse from memory. It's recited at brit milah, on Shabbat, and quoted constantly: "בְּרֵאשִׁית" is the name of a major newspaper supplement, a rabbinical school, and at least three cafés. The first letter ב is enlarged in every Torah scroll.

Genesis 1:1-5 — The First Day

The very first verses. Click any line to hear it. The pattern "vayomer Elohim... vayehi-khen" repeats through the chapter.

Genesis 1:26-28 — Creation of Humanity

The most contested three verses in human history. "B'tzelem Elohim" — in the image of God — is the foundational Jewish ethical claim.

Genesis 2:1-3 — Shabbat

The seventh day. These three verses are the textual basis for all of Shabbat law. Recited every Friday night during Kiddush.
