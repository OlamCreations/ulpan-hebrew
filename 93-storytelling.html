Telling a story in Hebrew means linking events in time, expressing surprise, building suspense, and landing the punchline. Israelis love a good "you won't believe what happened to me yesterday" — learn how to deliver one.

Cultural reality — A good Israeli story always involves : (1) a bureaucratic obstacle, (2) someone shouting, (3) an unexpected solution from a stranger, (4) ending with "אֵיזֶה אֶרֶץ" (what a country). It's the national narrative arc.
