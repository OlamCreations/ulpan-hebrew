Articles & Prepositions

Lesson 07 · ה' הידיעה ומילות יחס
Home 06 07 08

Hebrew has no indefinite article (a, an, some) — a bare noun is indefinite by default. The definite article (the) is a prefix: the letter ה (he) glued to the start of the word. Common prepositions (in, to, from, with, on) are also often prefixes.

Definite Article: ה־ (ha-)

To turn "a book" into "the book", glue ה (ha-) to the start of the word.

Inseparable Prepositions (Prefixes)

4 attached prepositions — בְּ (be, in/at) · לְ (le, to/toward/for) · מִ (mi, from/since) · כְּ (ke, like/as).

Separate Prepositions (Standalone Words)

Preposition + Article = Contraction

When preposition meets article — בְּ + הַ = בַּ (ba) · לְ + הַ = לַ (la) · כְּ + הַ = כַּ (ka). The ה of the article disappears, its vowel stays under the preposition.

Possessive: שֶׁל (shel — "of")
