Root ע־נ־ה — answering & affliction

Roots Atlas 25 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ע נ ה · the answering / affliction root
Home ע נ ה Morphology
ע נ ה
' · n · h
everything to do with answering — and a darker twin: affliction
10 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

This root holds two threads that share three letters but pull in opposite directions. The first is answering: to reply, to respond, the answer given. The second is affliction: to be bent low, hence poverty, torture, and the self-imposed lowering of fasting. Modern Hebrew keeps both alive, so the same skeleton ע-נ-ה gives you both "a reply" and "a fast day". Knowing they are historically distinct strands keeps you from forcing a false bridge. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַעֲנוֹתla'anot
simple active — the plain action
to answer, to reply
Pi'el
לְעַנּוֹתle'anot
intensive / specialized active
to torture, to torment
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְעַנּוֹתlehit'anot
reflexive / reciprocal — to / on oneself
to suffer; to fast

The two threads split across binyanim: pa'al carries "to answer", while the intensive pi'el "to torture" and its reflexive hitpa'el "to suffer / fast" carry the affliction sense (afflicting oneself = fasting). Same three letters, two unrelated families running through different moulds.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
