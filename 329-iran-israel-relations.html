Iran-Israel Relations

Lesson 329 · יְחָסֵי אִירָאן-יִשְׂרָאֵל
Home329

Few bilateral relationships compress as much history into so few decades as Iran-Israel. From the Pahlavi era covert alliance (Project Trident, oil-for-arms, Sayeret reconnaissance training) through the 1979 rupture, the proxy wars of Hezbollah/Hamas/Houthis, and today's nuclear standoff, the arc has reshaped the Middle East. Caught in the middle: Iranian Jews — Mashhadi, Tehrani, Isfahani — who carry both identities, sometimes loudly, often quietly. This lesson gives you the Hebrew vocabulary to read Kan Farsi broadcasts, follow IRGC sanctions news, understand dual-loyalty accusations, and navigate Persian-Israeli identity conversations in Israel.

Cultural note — The Persian Jewish community in Israel numbers around 250,000 and remains tightly knit. Many older Mashhadi families still speak Judeo-Persian at home, hold dual cultural memory of pre-1979 Tehran, and watch Kan Farsi (formerly part of Reshet Bet) — Israel\'s Persian-language broadcaster aimed at Iranians inside Iran. When the 2022 Mahsa Amini protests erupted, Persian-Israeli solidarity was immediate and visible. Conversely, "dual loyalty" accusations against Persian Israelis still surface in domestic discourse, especially during Iran-related crises. The vocabulary below is loaded — use it carefully.
