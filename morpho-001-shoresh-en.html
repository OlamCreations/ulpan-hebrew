The Root System

Morphology 01 · שֹׁרֶשׁ

Hebrew is not a language you memorize word by word. It is a machine. Almost every word is built from a root (שֹׁרֶשׁ, shoresh) — usually three consonants that carry the core meaning — poured into a pattern (a fixed mould of vowels and prefixes). The root gives the meaning; the pattern gives the part of speech and the nuance. Learn the moulds and one root unlocks a dozen words at once. This track teaches you to decode and derive, so a word you have never seen is rarely a closed door.

The method of this whole track — Every concept comes in three moves. 1. Decompose the word (prefix / root / pattern / suffix). 2. Isolate the pattern — the generative mould. 3. Snowball — three or four more words on the same mould, so you feel dozens unlock at once. The trigger for this whole idea was one word: בְּהַצְלָחָה (b'hatzlacha, "good luck") = preposition בְּ + the noun הַצְלָחָה (success), itself the hif'il action-noun mould הַ_ָ_ָה on the root צ-ל-ח. We unpack that fully in M4.

Anatomy of a root: כ־ת־ב (k-t-v = "writing")

Three consonants. Everything about writing comes from them. The vowels and affixes change; the three letters stay.

כָּתַב←כ ת ב+_ָ_ַ_
root k-t-v + the pa'al past mould → katav = "he wrote". The plainest verb.
מִכְתָּב←כ ת ב+מִ_ְ_ָָ
same root + the מ noun mould (often "the result / the thing") → mikhtav = "a letter" (the written thing).
הַכְתָּבָה←כ ת ב+הַ_ְ_ָָה
same root + the hif'il action-noun mould → hakhtava = "dictation" (causing writing). Recognize the mould? It is the בהצלחה family.

The full כ־ת־ב family

One root, eleven words. Tap any to hear it. None of these were memorized separately — they were derived.

Decode drill — find the root

Strip the prefixes, suffixes and vowels. What three consonants are left? That is the root, and the meaning lives there.
