Check Point — The Original Israeli Cyber Giant

Lesson 217 · צֶ'ק פּוֹינְט
Home217

Check Point Software was founded in 1993 by Gil Shwed (CEO), Marius Nacht, and Shlomo Kramer — all three Unit 8200 alumni. The company invented the commercial firewall (FireWall-1), which became the standard for enterprise security worldwide. Check Point IPO\'d on Nasdaq in 1996. Today it pulls roughly $2.5B annual revenue, employs around 6000 people, and is headquartered in Tel Aviv. Gil Shwed is the longest-serving founder-CEO in the cyber industry — over 30 years at the helm.

Cultural note — Check Point is "the original." Every Israeli cyber unicorn (Imperva, CyberArk, SentinelOne, Wiz) descends from this lineage either directly (founders worked at Check Point) or via the broader 8200 → cyber pipeline that Check Point pioneered. The Tel Aviv HQ on the Yarkon (Park HaYarkon area) employs ~3000. Gil Shwed\'s reputation: builds for the long term, hates the IPO-and-flip pattern, treats Check Point as a multi-decade institution. Israel\'s cyber industry is sometimes called "Check Point + descendants."
