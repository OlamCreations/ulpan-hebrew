Root ז־ר־ק: throwing & injecting

ז ר ק
z · r · k
everything to do with throwing, hurling, injecting, spraying out
5 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one motion: something is thrown out. Throw it by hand (the plain verb), let it be thrown (the passive), or force it under the skin (inject, "throw in"). The same hurling idea names the injection itself and the fountain that throws water into the air. Watch how the causative binyan specializes a wide everyday verb into a precise medical act. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִזְרֹקlizrok
simple active, the plain action
to throw, to hurl, to toss
Nif'al
לְהִזָּרֵקlehizarek
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be thrown
Hif'il
לְהַזְרִיקlehazrik
causative, make someone do it
to inject ("throw" a fluid in)

Notice the split of labour: pa'al is the broad "throw", nif'al its passive "be thrown", and hif'il narrows to the technical "inject", the causative made it a specialist. The pi'el, pu'al, huf'al and hitpa'el slots are not lexicalized for this root.
