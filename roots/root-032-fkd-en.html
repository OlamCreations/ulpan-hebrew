Root פ־ק־ד: commanding & counting

Roots Atlas 32 · שֹׁרֶשׁ פ ק ד · the commanding / counting root
Home פ ק ד Morphology
פ ק ד
p · k · d
everything to do with commanding, counting, and entrusting
9 words · 1 root · 4 verb-voices

This is a wide root, and the spread is the lesson. One ancient idea, to take charge of, fans out into giving an order, taking a count (a census), assigning a role, and entrusting a deposit. The officer who is in charge, the order he gives, the role you are assigned, the deposit you leave for safekeeping: all are the same act of "taking responsibility over" something. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִפְקֹדlifkod
simple active, the plain action
to command; to count; to visit; to remember
Nif'al
לְהִפָּקֵדlehipaked
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be counted; to be missing (of a soldier)
Pi'el
לְפַקֵּדlefaked
intensive / specialized active
to command, to be in charge (military)
Hif'il
לְהַפְקִידlehafkid
causative, make someone do it
to deposit; to check (baggage); to appoint

The same idea of "putting in charge" splits by binyan: pa'al gathers the ancient senses (command, count, visit, remember), pi'el is the military "to command", and hif'il is "to entrust / deposit / appoint". Nif'al is the passive "to be counted", which in army usage becomes "to be missing". The pu'al and huf'al are not lexicalized.
