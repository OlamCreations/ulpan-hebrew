Root ח־ד־ש: renewing / new / month

Roots Atlas 40 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ח ד ש · the new / renewing root
Home ח ד ש Morphology
ח ד ש
ch · d · sh
everything to do with newness and renewal
7 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root holds a small surprise: new and month are the same three letters. A month (חֹדֶשׁ) is named for the new moon, the moment the sky renews itself, so the calendar word is literally a renewal word. The verb makes things new again (innovate, renovate); the reflexive lets a thing renew itself (regenerate). Around them sit the adjective "new", the noun for an innovation, and the abstract act of renewal. Once you see that the month is just "the renewal", the whole family snaps together. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְחַדֵּשׁlechadesh
intensive / specialized active
to renew, to innovate, to modernise
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְחַדֵּשׁlehitchadesh
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to renew itself, to be renewed, to regenerate

This root lives in the intensive binyanim, not the simple pa'al: the everyday verb is the pi'el לְחַדֵּשׁ (to renew), with its reflexive hitpa'el for things that renew themselves. There is no common simple "to be new" verb; the adjective חָדָשׁ covers that state directly. Not every root uses the pa'al, and that is normal.
