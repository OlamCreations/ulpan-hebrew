Root ט־ע־ם: taste

ט ע ם
t · '· m
everything to do with taste
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: taste. On the tongue it is flavor; in the mind it stretches to the reason or sense of a thing (a ta'am for doing something), and in liturgy the same letters name the cantillation marks that give a verse its melodic accent. Pour the root into a verb mould and you taste; pour it into the causative and you make something stand out, you stress it. Every word here is the same three letters seen from a different angle, verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִטְעֹםlit'om
simple active, the plain action
to taste
Hif'il
לְהַטְעִיםlehat'im
causative, make someone do it
to stress, to emphasize (make stand out)

Only two binyanim are lexicalized here: the plain pa'al "to taste" and the causative hif'il, which moves the meaning from the tongue to attention, "to make a thing stand out", hence "to stress, to emphasize". The passives (nif'al, pu'al, huf'al) and the pi'el / hitpa'el are not in everyday use for this root. That is normal: not every root fills all seven moulds.

Words born from the root
