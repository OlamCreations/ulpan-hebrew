Root נ־ס־ע: travel

Roots Atlas 97 · שֹׁרֶשׁ נ ס ע · the traveling root
Home נ ס ע Morphology
נ ס ע
n · s · ' (ayin)
everything to do with traveling and being moved along
7 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root for motion across distance: traveling. The pa'al לִנְסֹעַ is the everyday verb for going by car, bus or train. Make it causative and you get לְהַסִּיעַ, to drive others, to transport. From there the family spreads into the traveller (נוֹסֵעַ), the journey itself (נְסִיעָה, מַסָּע), the act of ferrying people (הַסָּעָה), and even the machine that carries things along, a conveyor belt (מַסּוֹעַ). None of these are memorized separately. They are the same three letters seen from different angles. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִנְסֹעַlinsoa
simple active, the plain action
to travel, to ride, to drive (go by vehicle)
Hif'il
לְהַסִּיעַlehasia
causative, make someone do it
to transport, to drive (others), to ferry

The pa'al / hif'il pair is the heart of this root: לִנְסֹעַ is you going somewhere, לְהַסִּיעַ is you causing someone or something to go (the classic causative jump of the hif'il). Notice the third radical is ע (ayin), which pulls a furtive patach before it, hence the -oa / -ia endings. The other binyanim are not lexicalized here.
