Root ב־נ־ה: building

Roots Atlas 23 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ב נ ה · the building root
Home ב נ ה Morphology
ב נ ה
b · n · h
everything to do with building
9 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: building, raising a structure stone by stone. The verb erects, and the nouns name what is raised: the builder, the act of building, the building itself, even the abstract pattern a thing is built on. Hebrew grammar borrows this very word, binyan, for its verb-stems, because each binyan is a mould you pour a root into. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִבְנוֹתlivnot
simple active, the plain action
to build, to construct
Nif'al
לְהִבָּנוֹתlehibanot
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be built
Hif'il
לְהַבְנוֹתlehavnot
causative, make someone do it
to structure, to give structure to

Three of the seven binyanim are lexicalized here: the plain act (pa'al), its passive (nif'al), and the causative-shaping hif'il "to structure". The pi'el and hitpa'el slots are not filled for this root, which is normal: a root rarely occupies all seven moulds.

Words born from the root
