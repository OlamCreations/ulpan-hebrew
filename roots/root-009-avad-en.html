Root ע־ב־ד: working / serving

Roots Atlas 09 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ע ב ד · the working root
Home ע ב ד Morphology
ע ב ד
' · v · d
everything to do with work and service
10 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: labour. The same root that gives you to work also gives you the worker's task, the slave, the laboratory, and even the word for a fact. The thread is service: a job done, a thing worked on, a person bound to work. Pour ע־ב־ד into a verb mould and you act; pour it into a noun mould and you get the work, the workplace, or the worker. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַעֲבֹדla'avod
simple active, the plain action
to work; to operate, to function
Pi'el
לְעַבֵּדle'abed
intensive / specialized active
to work upon, to process
Hif'il
לְהַעֲבִידleha'avid
causative, make someone do it
to employ; to make work

Notice the voice ladder: pa'al is plain work, pi'el is intensive work done on something (processing raw material), and hif'il is causative work, making someone else work. The passive partners nif'al, pu'al and huf'al exist in the system but are not lexicalized as everyday verbs for this root.
