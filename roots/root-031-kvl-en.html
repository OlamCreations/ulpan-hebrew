Root ק־ב־ל: receiving

Roots Atlas 31 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ק ב ל · the receiving root
Home ק ב ל Morphology
ק ב ל
k · b · l
everything to do with receiving and accepting
11 words · 1 root · 4 verb-voices

The everyday verb here, לְקַבֵּל, is one of the first you learn: to receive, to get, to accept. But the root spreads in surprising directions. Kabbalah is literally "that which is received", the received tradition. A thing that "holds and takes in" becomes capacity, a capacitor. To set two things to face and accept each other gives parallel. And the plain pa'al, oddly, means to complain. One root, from a receipt to the divine tradition. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al

Pi'el

to receive, to get; to accept
Hif'il
לְהַקְבִּילlehakbil
causative, make someone do it
to be parallel (to); to compare
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְקַבֵּלlehitkabel
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to be received, to be accepted

The headline verb sits in pi'el (לְקַבֵּל), not pa'al, which is unusual; the pa'al here drifted to a separate sense, "to complain". Hitpa'el supplies the passive ("to be accepted"), and hif'il branches off into "to set parallel / to compare". The pu'al and huf'al are not lexicalized.
