Root ר־ח־ב: width

Roots Atlas 205 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ר ח ב · the width root
Home ר ח ב Morphology
ר ח ב
r · ch · v
everything to do with being wide, broad, open space
8 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: wide. From it Hebrew measures width, names the broad open street you walk down, the vast space you breathe in, and the act of expanding something. The same root gives the everyday word for a street, רְחוֹב, because a street is simply "the wide place" between the houses. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִרְחֹבlirchov
simple active, the plain action
to be wide, to widen, to spread out
Hif'il
לְהַרְחִיבleharchiv
causative, make someone do it
to widen, to expand something (cause to be wide)

The contrast is the whole lesson: the pa'al לִרְחֹב is a state, "to be wide", while the causative hif'il לְהַרְחִיב acts on an object, "to make it wide". Its action-noun הַרְחָבָה (expansion) appears in the groups below.
