Root א־ד־ה: vapour

Roots Atlas 102 · שֹׁרֶשׁ א ד ה · the vapour root
Home א ד ה Morphology
א ד ה
' · d · h
everything to do with steam and evaporating
4 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

A small, tight family built on one image: water turning to steam. The base noun אֵד is the vapour itself; from it the verbs describe the act of vaporizing, and by extension vanishing into thin air. This is a final-ה root, so the verbs end in -ות rather than the usual shape. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְאַדּוֹתle'adot
intensive / specialized active
to vaporize, to evaporate; to simmer, to poach
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְאַדּוֹתlehit'adot
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to evaporate; to disappear

The contrast is the whole point: pi'el (לְאַדּוֹת) is transitive, you make something evaporate, while hitpa'el (לְהִתְאַדּוֹת) is what the water does to itself: it evaporates, it vanishes. There is no everyday pa'al for this root.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
