Root ג־ר־ר: dragging & towing

Roots Atlas 240 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ג ר ר · the dragging root
Home ג ר ר Morphology
ג ר ר
g · r · r
everything to do with dragging something behind you
5 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

One physical gesture sits at the heart of this root: dragging, hauling a weight that resists. Pour the three letters into the pa'al and you drag (לִגְרֹר); into the nif'al and you are dragged along, often against your will. The same root gives the tow truck (גְּרָר) and, by metaphor, the drag of physics and the idea that one thing drags another after it as a consequence. This is a geminate root, the second and third letters are the same ר, so it stays compact and never grows a huge family. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִגְרֹרligror
simple active, the plain action
to drag, to haul, to tow
Nif'al
לְהִגָּרֵרlehigarer
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be dragged, to be towed
Pi'el
לְגָרֵרlegarer
intensive / specialized active
to grate (vegetables, cheese) (rare)

The core pair is pa'al (drag) and nif'al (be dragged), active and passive of the same pull. The pi'el לְגָרֵר exists but is rare and shifts sense toward "grate" (a kitchen action where you drag food across a blade). No hif'il, pu'al, huf'al or hitpa'el are lexicalized; geminate roots like this one stay lean.

Words born from the root
