Root ע־ו־ר: awakening

Roots Atlas 42 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ע ו ר · the awakening root
Home ע ו ר Morphology
ע ו ר
' · w · r
everything to do with waking, rousing, becoming alert
8 words · 1 root · 5 verb-voices

This is a hollow root: the middle letter is a vav that hides under the vowels, so the words look short and shifting. The core meaning is waking, and from waking comes alertness, and from alertness comes the small remark you make when you notice something (הֶעָרָה). A surprising branch (pi'el) goes the other way, to the failure of alertness: to blind. One root, the whole spectrum from wide awake to in the dark. Every entry is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לָעוּרla'ur
simple active, the plain action
to wake up, to rouse (mostly imperative)
Nif'al
לְהֵעוֹרlehe'or
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be awakened, to wake (rare)
Pi'el
לְעַוֵּרle'aver
intensive / specialized active
to blind, to make blind
Hif'il
לְהָעִירleha'ir
causative, make someone do it
to wake someone; to remark, to comment
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְעוֹרֵרlehit'orer
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to wake up (oneself); to arise (a feeling, a problem)

Notice the chain: hif'il means both "wake someone" and "make a remark", to comment is to wake the room to a point. Hitpa'el is the everyday "I woke up", and it also covers feelings and problems that arise. The odd one out is pi'el, "to blind": the same family that makes you alert can also be twisted to shut the eyes. Pu'al / huf'al are not lexicalized here.
