Root צ־ל־ח: succeeding

Roots Atlas 168 · שֹׁרֶשׁ צ ל ח · the succeeding root
Home צ ל ח Morphology
צ ל ח
tz · l · ch
everything to do with succeeding and getting through
6 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

The old sense of these letters is to get across: to ford a river, to come out the other side. From crossing came succeeding, because to pull a thing off is to get through it. The verb gives you both the simple act and the causative push; the nouns give you success itself, the lucky one, and, in a curious twist, the flat plate and the little flask. When you wish someone בְּהַצְלָחָה, you are wishing them a good crossing. Every word is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִצְלֹחַlitzloach
simple active, the plain action
to succeed (in doing something); to cross, to traverse
Hif'il
לְהַצְלִיחַlehatzliach
causative, make someone do it
to succeed, to make it work

In daily speech the hif'il לְהַצְלִיחַ has all but replaced the older pa'al: a modern speaker says הִצְלַחְתִּי ("I succeeded") far more than צָלַחְתִּי. The plain pa'al survives mainly in the literal sense of crossing. No passive or reflexive partners are lexicalized for this root.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
