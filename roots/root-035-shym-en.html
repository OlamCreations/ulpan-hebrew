Root שׂ־י־ם: putting / placing

Roots Atlas 35 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ש י ם · the putting / placing root
Home ש י ם Morphology
ש י ם
s · y · m
everything to do with putting something somewhere
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

This is a hollow root: the middle letter is a vowel (י), so the consonants you really feel are שׂ and ם. The core gesture is putting, placing, setting down. From the simple act of putting something on a table, the family stretches to placing a person in a job (assignment) and to the thing you are set to do (a mission). The hollow shape means the verb conjugates around a long vowel instead of three full consonants, which is why it looks short. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לָשִׂיםlasim
simple active, the plain action
to put, to put on, to place
Hif'il
לְהָשִׂיםlehasim
causative, make someone do it
to place, to put; to turn something into something

Hollow roots travel light: only pa'al and a hif'il are in active use here, and the two sit very close in meaning. The passive idea ("to be placed") is normally carried by a separate root, הוצב, rather than by a nif'al or huf'al of שׂ-י-ם. That is typical of weak roots, which rarely fill all seven binyanim.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
