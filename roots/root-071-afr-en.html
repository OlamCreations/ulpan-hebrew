Root א־פ־ר: ash & grey

Roots Atlas 71 · שֹׁרֶשׁ א פ ר · the ash-and-grey root
Home א פ ר Morphology
א פ ר
a · p · r
everything the colour of ash
4 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One image sits under these three letters: ash (efer), and the dull grey it leaves behind. From that colour Hebrew builds an adjective (grey), a verb (to turn grey), and then a surprise jump: makeup. Spread powder on a face and you are literally ashing it, le'aper. Notice how a single colour-word reaches from a burnt-out fire to a cosmetics counter. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְאַפֵּרle'aper
intensive / specialized active
to apply makeup to (a face)
Hif'il
לְהַאֲפִירleha'afir
causative, make someone do it
to turn / make grey

Only two binyanim are lexicalized here. Pi'el takes the colour and does something deliberate with it (paint a face), while hif'il describes the slow drift into grey. There is no everyday pa'al "to ash"; the root lives mostly as colour and cosmetics.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
