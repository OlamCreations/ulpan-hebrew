Root פ־נ־ה: turning / facing

פ נ ה
p · n · h
everything to do with turning toward
9 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

One body gesture sits under this whole family: turning to face something. Turn toward a person and you address them; turn a space empty and you clear it out; the spot where two walls turn is a corner; the moment a story turns is a turning point. Even פָּנִים, the face, is just the side you turn forward. Every word below is the same three letters caught mid-rotation, verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִפְנוֹתlifnot
simple active, the plain action
to turn, to face, to address (ל-)
Pi'el
לְפַנּוֹתlefanot
intensive / specialized active
to clear off, to evacuate (someone)
Hif'il
לְהַפְנוֹתlehafnot
causative, make someone do it
to direct, to refer (someone elsewhere)

Notice the family logic: pa'al is you turning yourself; pi'el is you turning a place empty; hif'il is you turning someone toward a destination. No nif'al or hitpa'el is lexicalized here, which is normal: a root rarely fills all seven binyanim.
