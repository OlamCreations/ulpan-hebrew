Root ג־ב־ר: strength

Roots Atlas 116 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ג ב ר · the strength root
Home ג ב ר Morphology
ג ב ר
g · v · r
everything to do with strength and overcoming
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: prevailing, growing stronger than what stands against you. The same letters give you the act of overpowering, the man who is strong, the hero, and the abstract quality of courage. Notice the thread: this is not raw force but the upper hand, strength measured against resistance. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִגְבֹּרligbor
simple active, the plain action
to grow stronger, to overpower (על)
Hif'il
לְהַגְבִּירlehagbir
causative, make someone do it
to boost, to amplify, to intensify

Only the simple pa'al (to prevail) and the causative hif'il (to amplify) are lexicalized here. The pa'al is the inner state of getting stronger; the hif'il pushes that strength onto something else, which is why it means "to turn the volume up". The other binyanim are not used for this root.

Words born from the root
