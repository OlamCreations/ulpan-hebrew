Root ס־כ־ם: summing & agreeing

ס כ ם
s · kh · m
everything to do with totals and agreement
8 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

Three consonants run one idea in two directions: bringing things to a total. Add up numbers and you reach a sum; add up a discussion and you reach a summary; bring two minds to the same total and you reach agreement. The same root that closes an arithmetic line closes a negotiation. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִסְכֹּםliskom
simple active, the plain action
to sum up, to complete (rare)
Pi'el
לְסַכֵּםlesakem
intensive / specialized active
to summarize, to conclude
Hif'il
לְהַסְכִּיםlehaskim
causative, make someone do it
to agree, to consent

Watch the split: the everyday verb for summarizing is the pi'el, while agreeing rides the hif'il ("to bring oneself to the same total"). The pa'al survives mostly in formal or literary use. The passives nif'al, pu'al, huf'al and the reflexive hitpa'el are not lexicalized here.

Words born from the root
