Root נ־ג־ש: approaching / accessing

Roots Atlas 39 · שֹׁרֶשׁ נ ג ש · the approaching / accessing root
Home נ ג ש Morphology
נ ג ש
n · g · sh
everything to do with drawing near and bringing forward
7 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

The core idea is coming near. The first letter נ is weak: it drops out of the pa'al infinitive, which is why "to approach" looks like לָגֶשֶׁת, with no נ in sight. The same root, in the hif'il, means to bring something near to someone, hence to serve food or to hand in a form. From there the family gives you access and a tray (what you bring food on), the quality of being approachable, and, when two things approach too hard, a collision. The disappearing נ is the puzzle of this whole family. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לָגֶשֶׁתlageshet
simple active, the plain action
to approach, to come near (ל־, אל)
Hif'il
לְהַגִּישׁlehagish
causative, make someone do it
to serve (food, drink), to hand in / hand over, to present

The weak initial נ makes the pa'al look irregular: it assimilates and vanishes, leaving לָגֶשֶׁת. In the hif'il it also drops, giving לְהַגִּישׁ with a dagesh in the ג that "remembers" the lost נ. The hitpa'el here is not reflexive of approaching but of clashing, the collision noun below. This is normal behaviour for first-נ roots.
