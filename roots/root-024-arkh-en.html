Root ע־ר־ך: arranging & value

Roots Atlas 24 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ע ר ך · the arranging / value root
Home ע ר ך Morphology
ע ר ך
' · r · kh
everything to do with arranging, valuing, editing
14 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One image sits under this whole family: laying things out in order. From arranging comes the setting up (a table, a meeting, a text you edit); from a thing's place in an order comes its value; and from sizing up where something stands comes estimating and appreciating it. Battle-lines, systems, exhibitions, even a math exponent: all are things lined up in a deliberate arrangement. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לַעֲרֹךְla'aroch
simple active, the plain action
to arrange, to prepare; to conduct (a meeting/event); to edit
Hif'il
לְהַעֲרִיךְleha'arich
causative, make someone do it
to estimate, to appraise; to appreciate, to respect

Two binyanim carry the verb load: the pa'al "to arrange / edit" and the hif'il "to estimate / appreciate", note how the causative step turns physical arranging into mental sizing-up. The passive and reflexive slots are not lexicalized for this root; Hebrew expresses being-arranged with other constructions.
