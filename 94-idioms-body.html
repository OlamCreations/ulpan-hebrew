Idioms with Body Parts

Lesson 94 · ביטויים עם איברי גוף
Home94

Hebrew is rich in idioms using body parts — heart, eye, hand, foot, head, mouth. Mastering 30 of these makes your Hebrew sound native instantly. Most are biblical or Talmudic in origin.

Cultural reality — When an Israeli says הָעֵינַיִם שֶׁלְּךָ גְּדוֹלוֹת מֵהַבֶּטֶן (your eyes are bigger than your stomach), they're not commenting on your anatomy — they mean you took too much food. Idioms are everywhere; literal translation will mislead you.
