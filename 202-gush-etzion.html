Gush Etzion — The Etzion Bloc

Lesson 202 · גּוּשׁ עֶצְיוֹן
Home202

Gush Etzion is a cluster of Jewish communities ~15km south of Jerusalem, founded in 1927 and refounded in 1967. The bloc fell to the Jordanian Arab Legion in May 1948, on the eve of Israeli Independence — a wound that shaped religious-Zionist memory for two generations. Today ~80,000 residents live across ~22 communities including Efrat, Alon Shvut, Bat Ayin, Tekoa, and the rebuilt Kfar Etzion. The "Lamed Heh" (the 35 fighters) convoy lost in January 1948 trying to reach the besieged kibbutzim is foundational mythology — a story every religious-Zionist child knows by heart.

Cultural note — Gush Etzion sits in a unique psychological position: within the pre-1967 Green Line geography but on the Israeli side of the security barrier, populated heavily by religious-Zionist olim including many Anglos and French. Yom Yerushalayim (Jerusalem Day) is celebrated here with extra intensity — many gather to sing "Hineh Lo Yanum Shomer Yisrael" at the Lone Tree (Etz Boded), the oak that survived the 1948 fall. If you want to feel religious-Zionist Israel in concentrated form, attend a Yom Yerushalayim ceremony in Alon Shvut.
