The Haredi Yiddish Register

Lesson 163 · יִידִישׁ חֲרֵדִי
Home163

Among ultra-Orthodox (haredi) Jews — especially Hasidim — Yiddish is still a living mother tongue. In Bnei Brak, Mea Shearim, and Stamford Hill, you'll hear Yiddish spoken on the street. This lesson covers the register: yeshiva vocabulary, daily Hasidic life, the special Yiddish grammar that's seeping into modern Hebrew through the haredi political class.

Cultural note — There are roughly 1.3 million haredim in Israel (~13% of the population). Hasidic groups (Gerer, Belz, Vizhnitz, Satmar, Chabad) often use Yiddish at home and yeshiva. Litvish (Lithuanian-tradition) haredim use Yiddish less, but it dominates yeshiva slang. Knowing this register opens up a whole layer of Israeli politics, demography, and humor.
