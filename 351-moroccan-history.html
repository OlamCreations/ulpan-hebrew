Ancient & Medieval Moroccan Jewish History

Lesson 351 · יְהוּדֵי מָרוֹקוֹ — אַלְפַּיִם שָׁנָה
Home351

Moroccan Jewry is not a footnote of the Spanish Expulsion. It is a 2000+ year continuous presence — predating Islam, predating the destruction of the Second Temple, rooted in Berber tribes and Roman trade routes. Understanding the distinction between Toshavim (the indigenous Berber-Jewish population) and Megorashim (the Spanish exiles arriving after 1492) is the key that unlocks every Moroccan Jewish family story, every mellah, every liturgical tradition. This lesson covers the ancient layer through the Saadian dynasty.

Cultural note — The phrase "Moroccan Jews are Sephardim" is a partial truth. The Megorashim are Sephardim (Iberian exiles). The Toshavim are something older — North African Jews whose presence predates Sepharad itself. In many Atlas Mountain villages, families traced descent to Berber tribes converted to Judaism centuries before Islam reached the Maghreb. When you meet a Moroccan Israeli today, ask whether their family was Toshavim or Megorashim — the answer often surprises them, and it changes the story.
