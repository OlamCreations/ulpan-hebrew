IDF Training — Tironut to Officer

Lesson 153 · טִירוֹנוּת
Home153

Every Israeli soldier starts with טִירוֹנוּת — basic training. Combat soldiers do tironut for 4 months at one of three bases (Mahane Tzeelim, BaHaD 4, or specific unit base). Then advanced training (אִמּוּן מִתְקַדֵּם) — 4 more months. Then squad leader (מַ"כ) course (3 months). Then maybe officer school (BaHaD 1) — 6 months. The training Hebrew is its own register.

Cultural note — "מַסָּע כּוּמְתָּה" — the beret march — is the climax of basic training. 50-90 km, depending on unit. Family meets the soldier at the end. The beret is awarded under Israeli flag, often at a meaningful site (Western Wall, Masada, Latrun). The phrase "סוֹף מַסָּע" — "end of march" — is shorthand for "I made it through."
