Root ח־ת־ן — marriage

Roots Atlas 89 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ח ת ן · the marrying root
Home ח ת ן Morphology
ח ת ן
ch · t · n
everything to do with marrying
4 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three consonants hold one social event: joining two families through marriage. The same letters give you the groom, the wedding, the act of marrying someone off, and the act of getting married yourself. Notice the side meaning hiding in plain sight: חָתָן is both the bridegroom and the son-in-law — because to marry into a family is to enter it. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְחַתֵּןlechaten
intensive / specialized active
to marry off (someone)
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְחַתֵּןlehitchaten
reflexive / reciprocal — to / on oneself
to get married (with) (עם)

The contrast here is the heart of the root. Pi'el לְחַתֵּן is what a parent or matchmaker does to others (marry them off); hitpa'el לְהִתְחַתֵּן is reflexive, what you do to yourself (get married). No pa'al verb is lexicalized for this root; the family lives almost entirely in pi'el, hitpa'el, and the nouns.
