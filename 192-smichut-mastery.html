Smichut & Possession Mastery

Lesson 192 · בְּעָלוּת בְּעִבְרִית
Home192

Smichut (construct chain) is Hebrew\'s essential way to express possession and association. Beyt-ha-sefer = "house of the book" = school. Sefer-ha-yeladim = "book of the children" = children\'s book. The first noun changes form (often with definite article rules), the second noun stays definite if the whole phrase is. Mastering smichut means natively producing complex Hebrew phrases without saying "shel" repeatedly. This is the biggest grammar marker between intermediate and advanced Hebrew.

Cultural note — Native Israelis use smichut constantly: rosh-ha-memshala (head of government), bet-cholim (hospital), sefer-ha-aviv (springbook), kol-ha-aretz (whole-the-country). Compounds where you\'d say "X of the Y" in English. The first word can change (sefer→sifrei) — that\'s the construct form. Plural smichut: bnei-isra\'el = "sons of Israel" = Israelis. Once you see this pattern, everything in the news makes sense.
