Moroccan Jews shaped Israel beyond the kitchen and the Mimouna table. Politics, intelligence, finance, literature, mysticism — the bench is deep. This lesson surveys the names you will hear in the news, in conversation, in synagogue, in the bookshop. Knowing them is part of being a Moroccan-tradition oleh in 2026 Israel.

Cultural note — The Moroccan-Israeli political ascent is a 50-year story. From the demonstrations of the Wadi Salib (Haifa, 1959) and the Black Panthers (Jerusalem, 1971) protesting Ashkenazi-establishment discrimination, to David Levy entering the cabinet (1977), to Aryeh Deri reshaping Sephardi politics with Shas (1984+), the Moroccan community moved from periphery to center. Today a Moroccan-origin Knesset member is unremarkable. That normalization itself is the achievement.
