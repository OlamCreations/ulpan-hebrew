Block 23 closes the healthcare section. Israel\'s universal healthcare combines kupot cholim (~98% coverage), public hospitals, supplemental insurance, and private practice. Lessons covered: 4 kupot, hospital network, doctor visits, prescriptions, mental health, MDA emergency, pharmacy, dental, imaging. Outcomes: life expectancy 83.5 years (top 10 globally), infant mortality 2.7/1000 (top 10), MRI per capita medium-high, mental health post-October 7 surged. This summary consolidates 10 essential terms, key institutions, key figures, ideological pairs.

Cultural note — Israeli healthcare is rated globally one of the most efficient — universal coverage at ~7.5% of GDP (vs US ~17%). Trade-offs: long ER waits, cramped wards, dental as private. Strengths: fast emergency response (MDA 7 min avg), digital infrastructure (e-prescriptions, kupa apps), highly trained pharmacists/doctors. After Block 23 you can navigate any kupa, hospital admission, doctor appointment, prescription pickup, dental clinic, MDA call. The vocabulary is more practical than political — use it daily.
