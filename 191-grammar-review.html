Advanced Grammar Review — All 7 Binyanim

Lesson 191 · שִׁבְעַת הַבִּנְיָנִים
Home191

Hebrew has seven verb patterns (binyanim) that systematically modify root meanings. Pa'al (active simple), Pi'el (intensive/causative), Hif'il (causative), Hitpa'el (reflexive/reciprocal), Nif'al (passive/middle), Pu'al (passive intensive), Hof'al (passive causative). Mastering all seven turns ~3000 root systems into ~21000+ functional verbs. This lesson reviews each binyan with examples, common irregular verbs, and the patterns that bind them. After this lesson you can read most Hebrew text without grammar guessing.

Cultural note — The 7-binyan system is what makes Hebrew elegantly compact. Once you see how כ-ת-ב (write) generates katav (he wrote, Pa'al), kitav (he addressed, Pi'el), hichtiv (he dictated, Hif'il), hitkattav (they corresponded, Hitpa'el), nichtav (it was written, Nif'al), kutav (it was addressed, Pu'al), huchtav (it was dictated, Hof'al), Hebrew opens up. Native Israelis don't memorize tables, they hear the pattern.
