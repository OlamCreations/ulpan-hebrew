everything to do with beauty
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root, one idea: beauty. The adjective יָפֶה (beautiful) is among the first words a learner meets, and יֹפִי is the everyday "great!" you hear all day in Israel. The same three letters become a verb two ways: in pa'al you become beautiful, in pi'el you make something beautiful. A small family, but it shows the system cleanly. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִיפוֹתlifot
simple active — the plain action
to become beautiful
Pi'el
לְיַפּוֹתleyapot
intensive / specialized active
to beautify, to embellish, to decorate

The pair reads like a rule of Hebrew: pa'al is the inner change (I grow beautiful), pi'el is the outward act on something else (I make it beautiful). The passives and the causative hif'il are not lexicalized for this root.
