Bukharan, Mountain Jewish, and Georgian Customs

Lesson 389 · מִנְהֲגֵי בּוּכָרִים, הָרָרִים וְגֵאוֹרְגִים
Home389

Three Jewish communities of the eastern Caucasus and Central Asia: Bukharan Jews (Uzbekistan, Tajikistan), Mountain Jews known as Juhuro (Dagestan, Azerbaijan), and Georgian Jews (Sakartvelo). Distinct from Sephardim and Ashkenazim, they preserve archaic Persian-Hebrew liturgy, warrior traditions, and life-cycle rituals largely unchanged since the Silk Road era. Today they form major communities in Israel — Bukharan in Jerusalem and Tel Aviv, Mountain Jews in Hadera and Or Yehuda, Georgian Jews in Ashdod and Lod.

Cultural note — These three communities are often grouped as "Edot Mizrach" (Eastern communities) but each has fierce distinct identity. A Bukharan grandmother will not appreciate being called Caucasian. A Mountain Jew (Juhuro) will correct you that they are not Georgian. Learn the distinctions: Bukharan = Tajik-Persian roots, Mountain Jew = Tat language warrior tradition, Georgian = oldest continuous community (since 6th century BCE allegedly). All three speak Hebrew today but home languages persist among elders.
