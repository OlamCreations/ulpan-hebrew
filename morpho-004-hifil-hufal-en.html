Hif'il & the בהצלחה family

This is the lesson that started the whole track. Hif'il is the causative binyan: it means "to make / cause someone to do" the root's action. Its action-noun mould, הַ_ָ_ָה, is one of the most productive word-factories in Hebrew, once you see it, dozens of everyday nouns fall open at once. We start by fully decoding the word that triggered all of this: בְּהַצְלָחָה.

Decoding בְּהַצְלָחָה ("good luck")

בְּהַצְלָחָה
בְּ, preposition "with / in".
הַצְלָחָה, the noun "success".
צ-ל-ח, the root (succeed / thrive).
הַ_ָ_ָה, the hif'il action-noun mould poured over the root.
So literally: "with success" → the everyday way to say good luck.
The chain to remember, verb → action-noun → phrase. הִצְלִיחַ (hitzliach, "succeeded") → הַצְלָחָה (hatzlacha, "success") → בְּהַצְלָחָה (b'hatzlacha, "good luck"). Every hif'il verb runs the same chain.

Recognizing hif'il itself, Past mould הִ_ְ_ִי_ with the long i (hitzliach, hidlik, hizmin, hisbir). Present mould מַ_ְ_ִי_ with a leading מ and i (matzliach, madlik, mazmin, masbir). The present participle often becomes an agent noun: מַדְרִיךְ (a guide), מַתְחִיל (a beginner), מַזְכִּיר (a secretary).
