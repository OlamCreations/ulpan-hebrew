Mapping the bridge between France and Israel through people. From Rabbi Sitruk who shaped 30 years of French Jewish identity, to Patrick Drahi who reshaped global telecom, to Bernard-Henri Levy who turned philosophy into political theater — these figures define what "Franco-Israeli" means in 2026. Knowing them means knowing the conversations you'll walk into in Jerusalem, Tel Aviv, Netanya, Ashdod. Half of these names will come up in your first month of small talk.

Cultural note — France-Israel ties run deeper than most realize. ~70K French olim arrived 2000-2024, concentrated in Netanya, Ashdod, Raanana, Jerusalem (Katamon, Bayit Vegan). The "francophone Israeli" identity has its own newspapers (Israel Magazine, LPH), radio (Kol HaShalom in French), neighborhoods, and political weight. Meyer Habib was Knesset member representing this diaspora. Many of these figures are controversial in France but mainstream in Israel — calibrate accordingly.
