Sarit Hadad — Queen of Mizrahi Pop

Lesson 242 · שָׂרִית חַדָּד
Home242

Sarit Hadad (born 1978, Hadera) is the female counterpart to Eyal Golan in Israeli Mizrahi pop. Caucasian-Mizrahi heritage. Career started age 14 with cassettes; broke into mainstream early 2000s. Her catalogue includes "Ke\'shehalev Boche" (When the heart cries) — an Israeli wedding standard, "Yom Shishi" (Friday) and "Tipat Mazal" (Drop of luck). She represented Israel at Eurovision 2002 and is a multi-instrumentalist (accordion, piano, oud) — visible at her concerts.

Cultural note — Sarit Hadad\'s "Ke\'shehalev Boche" (When the heart cries) is one of the most-played Israeli wedding songs ever — sung universally at huppot. Her vocal range and improvisational ornaments are distinctive. She also performs in Arabic, bridging the Mizrahi-Mediterranean cultural axis. Live she often switches to Arabic verses or duets with Arab Israeli singers. Knowing 2-3 of her songs equals standard wedding-circuit fluency.
