American Jewish History — Origins

Lesson 391 · הִיסְטוֹרְיָה יְהוּדִית אֲמֵרִיקָאִית
Home391

American Jewry is the second largest Jewish community in the world after Israel. Its story begins in 1654 when 23 Jewish refugees, fleeing the Portuguese reconquest of Recife in Brazil, landed in Dutch New Amsterdam (today New York). From those 23 to roughly 7 million today runs a story of three migration waves, religious reinvention, and an unprecedented experiment in Jewish life under conditions of full civic equality. This lesson gives you the Hebrew vocabulary to discuss that arc: the origins, the German Reform wave, the great Eastern European migration, the Lower East Side, and the postwar suburban transformation.

Cultural note — In Israel, American Jewish history is taught not as foreign history but as a parallel branch of the Jewish people. The relationship is dense: most Israelis have American relatives, the AIPAC and federation systems channel real political and financial weight, and the question of whether the American diaspora will hold its numbers (intermarriage, secularization) is a live policy concern. Knowing this history in Hebrew lets you discuss it with Israeli colleagues, advisors, and family without flattening it to clichés.
