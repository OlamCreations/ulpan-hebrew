Israeli cinema is a national therapy session on screen. Every major thematic current — Lebanon War trauma, the occupation, Mizrahi memory, religious-secular friction, Holocaust transmission — produced its own canon of films. Knowing the titles is one thing; understanding the Hebrew vocabulary that unlocks discussion in a Tel Aviv film club, a Jerusalem cinematheque Q&A, or a Sapir College class is another. This lesson gives you that vocabulary.

Cultural note — Israeli filmmakers rarely speak in single-issue terms. A film like Gett (2014) is at once feminist, religious-secular, Mizrahi, and procedural. The Band's Visit is Mizrahi, post-conflict, and lonely-in-the-Negev all at once. Don't expect the categories below to be airtight — they are entry points to a denser web. The cinematheques in Tel Aviv (Sderot Shaul HaMelech) and Jerusalem (Hebron Road) are where these conversations actually happen.
