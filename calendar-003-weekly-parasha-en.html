Weekly Parasha: The Torah Reading Cycle

פָּרָשַׁת הַשָּׁבוּעַ · 54 PARASHIYOT · 1 YEAR CYCLE · BERESHIT TO V'ZOT HABERACHA
Home Weekly Parasha
Each Shabbat in synagogue, a fixed weekly portion of the Torah, the parasha or sidra, is read aloud from the Torah scroll. The cycle starts on the Shabbat after Simchat Torah (late autumn) with Bereshit (Genesis 1) and ends one year later with V'zot HaBeracha (Deuteronomy 33-34, Moses' final blessing) on Simchat Torah itself. There are 54 parashiyot; in regular years some are doubled up so the whole cycle fits. The parasha is the central anchor of Jewish weekly time, sermons in synagogue, Shabbat dinner conversations, kids' school lessons all revolve around "this week's parasha."

♦ About the parasha cycle

The weekly parasha is the most powerful piece of Jewish chronological infrastructure ever built. By the time a Jewish child is 13 (bar/bat mitzvah age), they have heard the entire Torah read aloud 13 times. By 60, 60 times. The text becomes ambient, phrases from this week's parasha appear in conversations, news commentary, even Israeli politics ('this week we read about Joseph and his brothers' becomes a frame for current events).

How it works

Each week the parasha is divided into 7 sections, called 'aliyot' (ascents). Seven men are called up to the Torah on Shabbat morning, they don't read themselves; the baal koreh (master reader) chants for them. Each reading is preceded and followed by two short blessings the called-up person says. After the 7 aliyot, an 8th brief section (maftir) is read, then a Haftarah, a thematically linked passage from the Prophets.

The 54 parashiyot

Genesis (12): Bereshit, Noach, Lech-Lecha, Vayera, Chayei Sarah, Toldot, Vayetze, Vayishlach, Vayeshev, Miketz, Vayigash, Vayechi. Exodus (11): Shemot, Vaera, Bo, Beshalach, Yitro, Mishpatim, Terumah, Tetzaveh, Ki Tisa, Vayakhel, Pekudei. Leviticus (10): Vayikra, Tzav, Shemini, Tazria, Metzora, Acharei Mot, Kedoshim, Emor, Behar, Bechukotai. Numbers (10): Bamidbar, Naso, Beha'alotcha, Shelach, Korach, Chukat, Balak, Pinchas, Matot, Masei. Deuteronomy (11): Devarim, Vaetchanan, Eikev, Re'eh, Shoftim, Ki Tetze, Ki Tavo, Nitzavim, Vayelech, Ha'azinu, V'zot HaBeracha.

Doubled parashiyot

In regular (non-leap) years, ~7 pairs of parashiyot are read together to keep the 1-year cycle: Vayakhel-Pekudei, Tazria-Metzora, Acharei Mot-Kedoshim, Behar-Bechukotai, Chukat-Balak, Matot-Masei, Nitzavim-Vayelech. In leap years (13 months), these come apart and each is read separately.

Daily reading

Many Israelis follow a parashat hashavua daily routine: each weekday, read one aliyah of the upcoming week's parasha. By Friday afternoon, you've read the whole parasha once privately. Then Shabbat morning is the public hearing. Many apps (Sefaria, Pocket Torah) provide audio recordings of the cantillation.

Beyond Torah: Mishna + Daf Yomi

The parasha is the weekly Torah cycle. Two other cycles parallel it: Mishna Yomit (2 mishnayot/day, completes Mishna in ~6 years), and Daf Yomi (one folio of Talmud per day, completes Babylonian Talmud in 7.5 years, last Siyum HaShas was 2020 in Jerusalem, ~90,000 attended).
