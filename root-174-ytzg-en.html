Root י־צ־ג — presenting

Roots Atlas 174 · שֹׁרֶשׁ י צ ג · the presenting root
Home י צ ג Morphology
י צ ג
y · tz · g
everything to do with putting something in front of others
6 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

Three consonants carry one idea: placing something where it can be seen. From there the root fans out two ways at once: to show a thing (put it on display) and to represent someone (stand in their place). The same letters give you a theatre show, a computer screen, an art installation, and the lawyer who represents you. The initial yod is quiet in the verbs but resurfaces in the noun יִצּוּג. Every word is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְיַצֵּגleyatzeg
intensive / specialized active
to represent (act on someone's behalf)
Hif'il
לְהַצִּיגlehatzig
causative — make someone do it
to show, to present, to put on stage

The two verbs split the meaning cleanly: pi'el לְיַצֵּג is to represent (a lawyer, a delegate), while hif'il לְהַצִּיג is to present or display (a play, a slide, an argument). In the hif'il the first root letter yod assimilates and vanishes from the spelling, a normal trick of initial-yod roots. The passive and reflexive binyanim are not lexicalized here.

Words born from the root
