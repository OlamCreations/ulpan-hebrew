Block 22 closes the hi-tech section. Israel\'s tech industry runs on real numbers: about 10% of the population works in tech, around 50% of exports are tech, $20-80B in annual VC investment, 90+ unicorns. The pipeline goes 8200 → academy → startup → exit → reinvest, and it repeats. The lessons in this block covered Unit 8200, the VC ecosystem, Mobileye, Wix, Monday, Mellanox, Check Point, exit and IPO vocabulary, and the Tel Aviv tech corridor geography. This summary consolidates 10 essential terms, the key institutions, the figures who built the ecosystem, and the conceptual pairs that structure tech conversation.

Cultural note — Startup Nation is a real cultural fact, not just a marketing slogan. The vocabulary mixes Hebrew and English freely — "ya\'asu term sheet b\'shavua haba" (we\'ll do a term sheet next week) is a normal sentence. Knowing this lexicon means you can navigate Tel Aviv tech parties, read TheMarker tech section, follow Geektime, attend Cyber Week. It\'s also what most Israeli olim (especially from EU/US) eventually work in. Even if you don\'t code, knowing the vocabulary opens entry-level roles in marketing, BD, customer success, recruiting.
