Sigd — Beta Israel Festival of Renewal

Lesson 307 · סִיגֵּד
Home307

Sigd is Beta Israel\'s unique annual festival, celebrated 50 days after Yom Kippur (29th of Heshvan). It marks the renewal of covenant with God and the yearning for return to Jerusalem. Origins trace back to the 6th century in Ethiopia. Israel made it a state holiday in 2008 under PM Olmert. Commemoration: 24-hour fast, ascent to the highest mountain (in Ethiopia, the Hoharva), reading of Torah, prayers in Ge\'ez. In Israel: thousands gather at Tayelet Armon HaNatziv (Jerusalem promenade) overlooking the Old City.

Cultural note — Sigd is the only Israeli national holiday rooted in Beta Israel tradition rather than the mainstream Jewish calendar. The Sigd ceremony at Armon HaNatziv brings ~30K participants — kessim (priests) lead prayers in Ge\'ez, families pack picnics, traditional dances follow. Schools across Israel now teach Sigd alongside other holidays. The festival became a celebration of Ethiopian-Israeli identity proudly woven into the national fabric. Pnina Tamano-Shata as Aliyah Minister 2020-22 made the holiday more visible.
