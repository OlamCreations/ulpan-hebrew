Root ס־ב־ב: turning

Roots Atlas 146 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ס ב ב · the turning root
Home ס ב ב Morphology
ס ב ב
s · v · v
everything to do with turning, surrounding and going around
12 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

This geminate root (the ב doubles) spins one image into a remarkably wide family: turning, going around. From plain rotation comes everything that surrounds: the things around you become your environment, the people gathered around become a party, and what stands around an event becomes its circumstance. The deepest leap is causal: in older logic, what "brings something around" is its cause. Each word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לָסֹבlasov
simple active, the plain action
to revolve, to turn, to spin
Pi'el
לְסַבֵּבlesavev
intensive / specialized active
to cause; to surround
Hif'il
לְהָסֵבlehasev
causative, make someone do it
to turn / redirect (something); to change (something)

A clean geminate trio: pa'al is the intransitive spin (something turns), pi'el is transitive and pushes into "surround" and even "cause" (bring about), and hif'il redirects or converts something. The wandering / turning-around-oneself sense surfaces as the noun הִסְתּוֹבְבוּת (hitpa'el-type). The pu'al and huf'al are not lexicalized here.
