Football — Israeli Soccer Tribes

Lesson 231 · כַּדּוּרֶגֶל
Home231

Football (kaduregel) is Israel's most-followed sport. The top tier "Ligat HaAl" runs with 14 teams; below sit Liga Leumit and Liga Aleph. Three historical "tribes" structure fan loyalties: Hapoel (labor/Histadrut origin, red), Maccabi (Zionist/middle-class origin, yellow-blue), Beitar (Revisionist/right-wing origin, yellow-black). The national team competes in UEFA. Standout clubs: Maccabi Tel Aviv FC, Maccabi Haifa, Hapoel Be'er Sheva, Beitar Yerushalayim, Hapoel Tel Aviv.

Cultural note — Asking "what team do you support" reveals as much about an Israeli as their voting pattern. Beitar Yerushalayim's "La Familia" fans are notoriously right-wing (anti-Arab chants are an ongoing scandal). Hapoel fans tend left, secular, urban. Maccabi straddles. The Tel Aviv derby (Maccabi vs Hapoel TLV) is one of the world's oldest. Stadiums: Bloomfield (Tel Aviv), Sammy Ofer (Haifa), Teddy (Jerusalem). Most matches Friday afternoon (because Shabbat).
