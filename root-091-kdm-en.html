Root ק־ד־ם: before / forward

Roots Atlas 91 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ק ד ם · the before-and-forward root
Home ק ד ם Morphology
ק ד ם
k · d · m
everything to do with being ahead, earlier, or moving forward
11 words · 1 root · 4 verb-voices

One root spans space, time, and progress, because in Hebrew they are the same axis. קֶדֶם is the front, which is also the east (where the sun rises) and the ancient past (what came before). From this single "in front / earlier" idea grow verbs for preceding, doing things early, advancing something, and making progress. The forward direction in space becomes earliness in time becomes progress in life. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִקְדֹּםlikdom
simple active, the plain action
to precede; to be ahead
Pi'el
לְקַדֵּםlekadem
intensive / specialized active
to advance, to promote, to welcome
Hif'il
לְהַקְדִּיםlehakdim
causative, make someone do it
to do something early; to bring forward
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְקַדֵּםlehitkadem
reflexive / reciprocal, to / on oneself
to advance, to progress (intransitive)

Watch the voices line up. Pa'al = simply be ahead. Pi'el לְקַדֵּם = push something else forward (promote a project). Hif'il לְהַקְדִּים = causative on time, make it happen earlier. Hitpa'el לְהִתְקַדֵּם = the action turned back on yourself, you advance. The passive binyanim are not lexicalized for this root.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
