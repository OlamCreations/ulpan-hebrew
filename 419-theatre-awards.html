Israeli theatre is mapped by its prizes. The Israel Prize crowns lifetime achievement, the Annual Theatre Prize structures the season, and the press shapes which productions reach the public memory. Knowing the awards landscape is the fastest way to read the history of Hebrew theatre — every important name has a row in one of these lists.

Cultural note — Press reception in Israel is concentrated and concentrated quickly. A premiere at Habima or Cameri triggers the same evening reviews in Haaretz Tarbut Vesifrut, Yedioth Ahronoth and Maariv, then a second wave on Habama and Time Out Tel Aviv within 48 hours. A bad opening night kills a production within a week. A Pras Theatron HaShnati nomination, by contrast, can extend a run by a full season.
