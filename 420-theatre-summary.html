Lesson 420 · סִכּוּם תֵּיאַטְרוֹן יִשְׂרְאֵלִי

Closing Block 42 with a review of Israeli theatre from Habima 1917 Moscow to Gesher 1991 and Levin posthumous Israel Prize. You now have the vocabulary to navigate a Habima season program, follow a Hanoch Levin retrospective, read a review in Haaretz, attend the Acco Festival, or hold a conversation with an Israeli actor about Vakhtangov, Tzemach, and the strange birth of a Jewish theatre in revolutionary Moscow.

Cultural note — Israeli theatre is unusually political and unusually personal. A single Hanoch Levin play can savage a prime minister, mock a funeral, and break your heart in the same hour. Habima carries the founding myth (Moscow, Stanislavsky, Vakhtangov, the Dybbuk that astonished Europe). Cameri carries the everyday Israeli voice. Gesher carries the post-Soviet wound. Khan carries Jerusalem. Tmuna carries the experimental fringe. Knowing these five lets you read the season programs of any Israeli city and place yourself in the conversation.
