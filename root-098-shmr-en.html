Root ש־מ־ר: guarding

Roots Atlas 98 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ש מ ר · the guarding root
Home ש מ ר Morphology
ש מ ר
sh · m · r
everything to do with guarding, keeping and preserving
2 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root holds the whole idea of keeping something safe: to guard it, to maintain it, to preserve it. The pa'al לִשְׁמוֹר is everyday Hebrew for watching over, keeping a promise, or keeping Shabbat. Shift to the pi'el לְשַׁמֵּר and the keeping becomes deliberate, technical preservation: food, heritage, a state of things. The same three consonants spread out into a whole world of guards, watches and preservatives, but the two verbs below are the verified core. They are the same three letters seen from two angles. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִשְׁמוֹרlishmor
simple active, the plain action
to guard; to keep, to watch over, to maintain (על)
Pi'el
לְשַׁמֵּרleshamer
intensive / specialized active
to preserve, to conserve, to maintain (deliberately)

The pa'al / pi'el contrast is the lesson here. Pa'al לִשְׁמוֹר is the plain, broad act of keeping (guarding, observing, keeping a promise). Pi'el לְשַׁמֵּר intensifies it into deliberate preservation: preserving food, conserving heritage, maintaining a system. Hebrew has not lexicalized the other binyanim for this root, which is normal: not every root fills all seven.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.

Decode it yourself

Strip the prefixes, suffixes and vowels. Three consonants remain, and the meaning lives there.
