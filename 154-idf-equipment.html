Lesson 154 · צִיוּד וְתוֹרָה
Home154

Israeli weapons systems are mostly Israeli-designed: Merkava tank, Tavor rifle, Iron Dome, F-35I "Adir." The doctrine emphasizes speed, surprise, technology, and quality over quantity. "Purity of arms" (טוֹהַר הַנֶּשֶׁק) — restraint with civilian populations — is the core doctrinal principle, codified in the IDF Code of Ethics. Decoding equipment vocabulary lets you read any defense article.

Cultural note — The Merkava tank ("chariot") was designed in Israel after de Gaulle\'s 1967 weapons embargo. Its unique feature: engine in front (not back) to protect the crew. The Tavor was designed to replace M16 — bullpup design, optimized for Israeli urban combat. The F-35I "Adir" is the only F-35 in the world allowed customization (Israeli-specific software). The doctrine also centers on the "Dahiya doctrine" — disproportionate response to deter further attacks (controversial).
