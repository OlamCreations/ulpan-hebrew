Root ג־ש־ם — rain & making real

Roots Atlas 244 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ג ש ם · the rain & realization root
Home ג ש ם Morphology
ג ש ם
g · sh · m
everything from rain falling to a dream coming true
5 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

The starting point is the most concrete thing in a dry land: גֶּשֶׁם, rain. From rain — water that becomes physical, that touches the ground — Hebrew built the idea of the material (גַּשְׁמִי, corporeal) and then, beautifully, the idea of making a thing real: to fulfil a dream, to implement a plan (לְהַגְשִׁים), and for that dream to materialize on its own (לְהִתְגַּשֵּׁם). One root reaches from the weather report to the deepest word for realizing your vision. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Hif'il
לְהַגְשִׁיםlehagshim
causative — make someone do it
to fulfil, to realize, to implement
Hitpa'el
לְהִתְגַּשֵּׁםlehitgashem
reflexive / reciprocal — to / on oneself
to come true, to materialize

The verbal life of this root is the hif'il / hitpa'el pair, both abstract and both built off the "make material" idea. לְהַגְשִׁים is causative: you make the dream real. לְהִתְגַּשֵּׁם is its reflexive mirror: the dream makes itself real, it comes true. There is no everyday pa'al "to rain" from this root (Hebrew uses other phrasing); the concrete גֶּשֶׁם stays a noun.
