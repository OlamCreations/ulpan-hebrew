Mellanox — Networking Chips that Power AI

Lesson 216 · מֵלָנוֹקְס
Home216

Mellanox Technologies was founded in 1999 in Yokneam by Eyal Waldman, Roni Ashuri and others — many of them 8200 alumni. The company built InfiniBand and Ethernet networking chips, switches and cards, used in supercomputers, data centers and AI clusters. NVIDIA acquired Mellanox in 2020 for $6.9B — at the time the second-largest Israeli tech exit ever. Today Mellanox is the foundation of NVIDIA's AI datacenter networking stack (NVLink, BlueField DPU, ConnectX cards).

Cultural note — Mellanox is the rare Israeli tech success that\'s not a software company — semiconductors, hardware, real engineering. Yokneam Illit (north of Haifa) is sometimes called "Israel\'s Silicon Valley" — Mellanox, Marvell, Applied Materials and Intel Israel all have major sites there. Eyal Waldman\'s bet on InfiniBand against the broader Ethernet trend looked wrong in 2005 — and was vindicated by AI training workloads from 2017 onward. The NVIDIA-Mellanox deal closed during early COVID and reshaped global AI infrastructure.
