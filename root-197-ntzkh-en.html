Root נ־צ־ח — winning & permanence

Roots Atlas 197 · שֹׁרֶשׁ נ צ ח · the victory root
Home נ צ ח Morphology
נ צ ח
n · tz · ch
everything to do with winning, lasting forever, and leading the orchestra
7 words · 1 root · 2 verb-voices

One root, two faces that turn out to be the same face. נ־צ־ח means to prevail — and what prevails is what lasts. So the same letters give you victory (winning the battle) and eternity (winning against time). A surprising third sense rides along: the conductor who leads an orchestra is, in Hebrew, the one who makes the music prevail. Win, endure, conduct: three readings of the act of coming out on top.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְנַצֵּחַlenatze'ach
intensive / specialized active
to win, to beat; to conduct (an orchestra)
Hif'il
לְהַנְצִיחַlehantzi'ach
causative — make someone do it
to perpetuate, to commemorate, to immortalize

The active pa'al is not the lexical home of this root: the everyday verb "to win" lives in pi'el (לְנַצֵּחַ). The hif'il then adds the causative twist, to make something last forever. The passive binyanim are simply not lexicalized here.
