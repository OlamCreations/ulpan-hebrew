Lesson 400 closes Block 40 (lessons 391-399, American-Anglo Jews) AND the entire Blocks 31-40 Ethnic Heritages decade. From 1654 — when 23 Sephardic refugees from Recife landed in New Amsterdam — to roughly 7 million Jews in the United States today (the second-largest Jewish community in the world after Israel), American Jewry built denominational pluralism (Reform, Conservative, Modern Orthodox, Reconstructionist, Renewal, Haredi, Chabad), powered Israel\'s diplomatic and financial backbone ($3.8B annual aid, AIPAC, Birthright), and shipped roughly 250K Anglo olim post-1948 — Modi\'in, Beit Shemesh, Efrat, Ra\'anana, Jerusalem. Then October 7, 2023 changed everything: a 360% rise in U.S. antisemitism in 2024, generational rupture, and waves of Anglo aliyah inquiries. This lesson closes the decade. Next: Arts & Culture.

Decade close — Blocks 31-40 Ethnic Heritages — You\'ve walked through Mizrahi, Sephardi, Yemenite, Ethiopian, Russian, Bukharian, French, Latin American, South African and now American-Anglo Jewry. Ten communities. Ten languages of suffering and survival. The lesson under all of them: Klal Yisrael (כְּלָל יִשְׂרָאֵל) is not a metaphor. It\'s a network of memory, halakha, and movement. Your alyah joins this network — not as a stranger, but as the next link. From here, Block 41 opens Arts & Culture: the songs, films, novels, paintings and plays that emerged from this same network. The heritage is the soil. The art is the harvest.
