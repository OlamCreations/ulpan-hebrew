Sephardi-Ladino Music Tradition

Lesson 377 · מוּזִיקָה סְפָרַדִּית-לָדִינוֹ
Home377

The Sephardi musical tradition carries a millennium of memory: medieval Spanish ballads (romanzas) preserved in Ladino across the Ottoman diaspora, daily-life kantikas, Salonika paytan-craft fused with Ottoman maqamat, and a modern Israeli revival led by Yehoram Gaon, Yasmin Levy, Sarah Aroeste, and others. This lesson maps the genres, the canonical songs, the instruments, and the people who keep the repertoire alive in Israel today.

Cultural note — When Sephardim were expelled from Spain in 1492, they took the songs with them. For 500 years, the romanzas traveled by mouth from Salonika to Sarajevo to Jerusalem, sung at weddings, deathbeds, and cradles. The melodies absorbed Ottoman maqam, Greek rebetiko, Bulgarian rhythms — but the Castilian-rooted Ladino lyrics held. Today an old Sephardi grandmother in Bat Yam may sing a tune that crossed seven empires intact.
