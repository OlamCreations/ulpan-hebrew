Oligopolies & The Big Families

Lesson 136 · אוֹלִיגַרְכִיוֹת
Home136

Israel\'s domestic economy is dominated by ~10 family-controlled conglomerates that together control banks, supermarkets, food, telecom, insurance, and energy. Tnuva (dairy, ~70%), Strauss (snacks, coffee), Osem (pasta, instant), the Wertheimer family (IMI), the Tshuva family (Delek/gas/Nobel Energy), Ofer Holdings (real estate). The 2011 Cottage Cheese protests were aimed at this structure. The 2013 "Concentration Law" tried to break it up. Slowly progressing.

Cultural note — Most Israelis can name the controlling families of major conglomerates the way Americans know who runs Disney. The phrase "מִשְׁפָּחוֹת בַּעֲלֵי הַהוֹן" — "the controlling-capital families" — appears in news constantly. Sharon vs. the "tycoonim" was a 2003 reform episode; Lapid vs. the food monopoly was 2014; the 2011 Cottage Cheese boycott (~600K Israelis on Facebook in 6 weeks) is the modern reference point.

The Cottage Cheese Boycott — Facebook Post, June 2011

A typical viral post that triggered the 2011 boycott. Cottage cheese had risen from ~5 NIS to ~8 NIS over a few years. Within 6 weeks, 600K Israelis joined the boycott. Tnuva\'s sales dropped 40%. Prices rolled back.

Newspaper Headline — Concentration Law Coverage

Sample of business-page Hebrew about anti-concentration efforts. The 2013 law forced major holding companies to choose: financial OR real, not both. Took 5+ years to implement.
