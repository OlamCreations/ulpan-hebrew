Smichut (Construct State)

Lesson 25 · סמיכות
Home25

Smichut (סְמִיכוּת) is one of Hebrew's most distinctive features — a way to combine two nouns into a single concept by linking them. Like English compounds: "house door" = "front door" without "of". בֵּית סֵפֶר (beit sefer) literally "house [of] book" = school.

How it works — The first noun (nismach) often shortens. The second noun (somech) keeps its form. בַּיִת + סֵפֶר → בֵּית סֵפֶר. The whole pair is treated as one compound word.
Definite article rule — In smichut, only the SECOND word takes ה (the article). בֵּית הַסֵּפֶר (beit ha-sefer) "the school" — not "ha-beit ha-sefer".
