Russian Press — Israeli Russian-Language Media

Lesson 284 · תִּקְשֹׁרֶת רוּסִית בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל
Home284

Israel has the world's largest Russian-language press outside Russia: Vesti (daily, founded 1992), Novosti Nedeli (weekly), Russian Channel 9 (TV), Russkii Novosti (online), MK-Israel (Moskovskii Komsomolets). Total Russian-language readership reaches roughly 250-400K daily, peaking in late 1990s. Major topics include Russian-Israeli political affairs, FSU politics, Putin and Ukraine war coverage, and Russian-Jewish heritage. Channel 9 is the dedicated Russian-language TV channel, broadcasting since 2002.

Cultural note — Russian-language press in Israel reads differently than Hebrew press: more Russian/FSU politics, more pro-Russia or anti-Putin perspectives, more Russian high culture coverage, more conservative on social issues. Channel 9 broadcasts Russian-language news, talk shows, Russian movies subtitled. Many older Russian-Israelis (60+) consume only Russian-language media; the 1.5 generation consumes both. The Putin-Ukraine war (Feb 2022+) accelerated some declines as second-generation Russian-Israelis question heritage.
