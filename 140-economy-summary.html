The Israeli Economy — Synthesis

Lesson 140 · סִכּוּם הַכַּלְכָּלָה
Home140

Closing Block 14 with synthesis: 5 paradoxes that define the Israeli economy. (1) Top-tier high-tech + bottom-tier service productivity. (2) High GDP per capita + high cost of living. (3) Strong exports + weak domestic competition. (4) Low debt-to-GDP + huge government concentration. (5) Innovation leadership + chronic housing crisis. Once you understand these, every economic news story makes sense.

Cultural note — Israelis joke that the country has "two economies in one body." A 30-year-old software engineer in Herzliya makes $150K and complains about taxes. A 30-year-old waiter in Tel Aviv makes minimum wage + tips and lives with parents. Both are "Israeli middle class." This is the structural reality every economic-policy debate orbits.
