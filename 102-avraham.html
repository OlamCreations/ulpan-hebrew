Avraham — The First Patriarch

Lesson 102 · אַבְרָהָם
Home102

Genesis 12-22. Avraham leaves Ur, gets the covenant, fathers Yishmael with Hagar, then Yitzchak with Sara. The story climaxes at the Akeda — the binding of Isaac on Mount Moriah, the site of the future Temple in Jerusalem. Israelis name streets, neighborhoods, hospitals, and military operations after these characters. Words tagged [modern] are still in daily use.

Cultural note — "הִנֵּנִי" (hineni — here I am) is the moral keystone word of Jewish tradition. It's how Avraham answers God three times in the Akeda. It's also the standard formal way to volunteer for a hard task today. Yitzhak Rabin used it in his last speech: "אני, מספר רבים אבי הנני" — "I, the speaker, am here."

Akeda Vocabulary

Lech Lecha — Genesis 12:1-3

God's first call to Avraham. The phrase "lech lecha" is famously double — "go for yourself / to yourself." Reading: every Shabbat in late October.

The Akeda — Genesis 22:1-12 (excerpt)

The binding of Isaac. Read on Rosh Hashana every year. The text is sparse and dense — every word counts. Note three times Avraham says הִנֵּנִי.
