Root פּ־ר־ט: the individual detail

Roots Atlas 170 · שֹׁרֶשׁ פ ר ט · the detail root
Home פ ר ט Morphology
פ ר ט
p · r · t
everything that breaks a whole into single parts
9 words · 1 root · 3 verb-voices

One idea threads this whole family: taking a lump and breaking it into single units. Break a banknote into coins, break a topic into points, break a state-owned firm into private hands, even pluck single strings on a guitar. From that act come the noun for one detail (and one individual), the adjective private, the itemized breakdown, the menu of separate dishes, and the word for privatization. Same three letters, seen from the angle of 'the single part'. Every word is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִפְרֹטlifrot
simple active, the plain action
to change (cash into smaller units); to pluck (a string)
Pi'el
לְפָרֵטlefaret
intensive / specialized active
to detail, to itemize, to spell out
Hif'il
לְהַפְרִיטlehafrit
causative, make someone do it
to privatise

Watch the three binyanim split the idea: pa'al physically breaks the whole (cash, strings); pi'el breaks a topic into listed details; hif'il breaks public property into private parts. Each binyan applies the same 'break into singles' move to a different field. The passive partners are not lexicalized for daily use.
