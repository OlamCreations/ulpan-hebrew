Block 46 traced the Israeli dance world across nine lessons: Batsheva (1964) and the Naharin / Gaga era; Inbal (1949) with its Yemenite roots; the Suzanne Dellal Center as flagship dance house; folk dance (rikudei am) from Gurit Kadman to weekly harkadot; classical ballet (Israel Ballet 1968) and the Soviet-aliyah technique transfer; the contemporary independent ecosystem (Vertigo, KCDC, Yasmeen Godder, Pinto/Pollak); the academy training pipeline (Telma Yellin, Jerusalem Academy); the deep working vocabulary of class commands, programme texts, and reviews. This lesson consolidates seven essential names plus ten essential terms.

Cultural note — Israeli dance is one of the country\'s most consistent international cultural exports — Batsheva tours 50-70 cities a year, Vertigo and KCDC are programmed across Europe annually. The vocabulary in this block is the working vocabulary of dance professionals, parents of dance students, and serious audience members. Knowing it places you inside a community of perhaps 30,000 active dancers and the wider half-million Israelis who attend dance regularly.
