Block 29 closes the Russian-Israeli section. The 1990s wave of about a million olim reshaped Israel into the country it is today — Russian-Israelis make up roughly 17% of the population and dominate tech, classical music, science, and chess. Lessons covered the wave, FSU heritage, Yisrael Beiteinu politics, Russian press, Russian-language schools, integration generations, code-switching, the Russian accent, and the religious community. After Block 29 you can navigate Russian-Israeli social circles, identify generation status, follow Lieberman politics, recognize Russian-accented Hebrew, and appreciate the cultural blend.

Cultural note — The Russian-Israeli community is Israel's most successful case study of mass aliyah integration: arrived poor, integrated within one to two generations, now contributes ~17% of population and ~25% of tech, dominates classical arts. Knowing this lexicon means navigating Bat Yam, Ashdod, Ariel, understanding 1.5 generation friends, recognizing classical-music-loving Russian-Israeli colleagues. Combined with Block 28, you understand the full diaspora-to-Israeli pipeline.
