The Persian-Israeli community (~250K, mostly arrived 1948-1979) produced presidents, IDF chiefs, novelists, singers, and tech founders. This lesson maps the most notable figures across five domains. Understanding who they are unlocks cultural references in Hebrew media — from Knesset debates citing Katsav, to Rita concerts on Yom HaAtzmaut, to Mofaz appearing on news panels.

Cultural note — Iranian Jews ("Parsim" or "Yehudei Iran") are one of Israel\'s oldest diaspora communities, with roots going back 2,700 years to the Babylonian exile. They speak Judeo-Persian (Dzhidi) and Hebrew, and many families maintain Persian cuisine, music, and the celebration of Mimuna-style Sephardic rituals adapted from their heritage. Politically, the community leans center-right and Likud-friendly, though notable figures span the spectrum (Mofaz Kadima/centrist, Katsav Likud).
