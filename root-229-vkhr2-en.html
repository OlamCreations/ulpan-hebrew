Root ב־כ־ר: firstborn

Roots Atlas 229 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ב כ ר · the firstborn root
Home ב כ ר Morphology
ב כ ר
b · k · r
everything to do with being first and given priority
4 words · 1 root · 1 verb-voices

Careful: this root looks like its cousin ב־ח־ר (choosing) but it is a different family entirely. Here the idea is being first: the firstborn child, the ripe first fruit, the one who comes before the rest. From "first" grows "foremost", hence the senior official, and the act of putting one thing ahead of another, preference. The rare verb "to prefer" sits at the centre. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pi'el
לְבַכֵּרlevaker
intensive / specialized active
to prefer, to favour (rare / literary)

The verb is rare and literary: in everyday Hebrew "to prefer" is usually לְהַעֲדִיף (root ע־ד־ף), not this one. The living core of ב־כ־ר is nominal, the firstborn and the senior, while the verb stays on the shelf. Not every root lives mainly through its verb.
