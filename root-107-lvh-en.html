Root ל־ו־ה — accompanying, lending

ל ו ה
l · v · h
everything to do with going alongside — accompanying, borrowing, lending
7 words · 1 root · 4 verb-voices

One image holds this whole family together: walking beside. To accompany someone is to walk beside them; to lend money is to let it walk beside the borrower until it returns; to borrow is to take that money along with you. From the same three letters Hebrew builds an escort, a satellite circling its planet, and even a funeral — the last accompaniment of all. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

to borrow (to take a loan)
Nif'al
לְהִלָּווֹתlehilavot

to accompany, to join (to go along with)
Pi'el
לְלַוּוֹתlelavot
intensive / specialized active
to accompany, to escort
Hif'il
לְהַלְווֹתlehalvot
causative — make someone do it
to lend (to cause money to walk with another)

Notice how the binyanim split the meaning cleanly: pa'al takes the loan (borrow), hif'il gives it (lend, the causative "make it go to someone"), and pi'el / nif'al carry the physical sense of going alongside someone. The passive moulds pu'al and huf'al are not lexicalized here.

Words born from the root

Same root letters, different noun moulds (mishkalim). The mould tells you the function: who does it, the act of doing it, or the thing produced.
