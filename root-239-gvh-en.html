Root ג־ב־ה: height & collecting

Roots Atlas 239 · שֹׁרֶשׁ ג ב ה · the height root
Home ג ב ה Morphology
ג ב ה
g · v · h
everything to do with being high, and, oddly, collecting what is due
8 words · 1 root · 5 verb-voices

Three consonants hold two threads that feel unrelated until you look closely: height and collecting a debt. The link is the raised hand of the one who is owed, to be high is גָּבֹהַּ, and to collect taxes is לִגְבּוֹת, the tax collector standing over you. From the same root come the eyebrow (גַּבָּה, the high arch above the eye) and the noun גֹּבַהּ, sheer altitude. The vowels decide whether you rise, raise something, or reach out for what you are owed. Every word here is verified against pealim, niqqud included.

The verb across the binyanim (voices)

The same root runs through the verb system. Each binyan is a voice: it bends the one idea into active, passive, causative or reciprocal. Tap to hear.

Pa'al
לִגְבּוֹתligvot
simple active, the plain action
to collect (dues, taxes, debt)
Pa'al
לִגְבֹּהַּligboah
simple active, the plain action
to be high, to be exalted
Nif'al
לְהִגָּבוֹתlehigavot
passive / middle, it happens to the subject
to be collected (of tax, fees)
Pi'el
לְגַבּוֹתlegabot
intensive / specialized active
to back up, to support
Hif'il
לְהַגְבִּיהַּlehagbiah
causative, make someone do it
to raise, to lift, to elevate

This root carries two unusual features. First, the pa'al fills two distinct senses: לִגְבּוֹת (to collect what is owed) and לִגְבֹּהַּ (to be high), the height thread and the debt thread sharing one binyan. Second, the hif'il לְהַגְבִּיהַּ is the everyday causative "raise it higher". The passive pu'al and huf'al are not lexicalized here; nif'al covers "to be collected".

Words born from the root
